# Reflection 006 — Cross-Compatibility Routing-Surface Architecture

*Cycle 7 RESEARCH close. 2026-05-21.*

The cycle began with three linked architectural questions about the chat-completions routing surface — where the decision lives, how I/O contracts are enforced, what fallback shape applies. It closes with an architectural recommendation (tiered hybrid + ADR-027 escalation) that neither the original framing nor the post-Phase-A reframe predicted. The path from there to here is the cycle's actual story, and most of what is worth reflecting on lives in that path rather than in the destination.

## Two reframings, asymmetrically managed

Two significant reframings occurred during RESEARCH. The first was agent-initiated and validated empirically. The second was practitioner-initiated and verified structurally. The asymmetry between how each was handled is itself a methodological signal.

Movement one came at the end of Phase A. After the empirical work established that NL-routing under production tool-rich clients converges on zero ensemble dispatch, the agent proposed a strong reframe — *the OpenAI `tool_choice` parameter is the existing forced-routing mechanism; Q1 reduces to contract conformance*. The reframe was attractive. It dramatically simplified the design space. It collapsed the cycle's substantial Q1 work to documentation. It had local empirical support (qwen3:14b validated the mechanism). And it was the kind of clean answer that, in absence of the validation-spike discipline, would have crystallized into the Essay-Outline as the cycle's central finding.

The validation spike was the corrective mechanism. Spike λ confirmed the qwen3:14b case, and the user then authorized the paid MiniMax M2.5 follow-up. Spike λ-paid produced the counter-finding: the production model does not honor `tool_choice={"name":"invoke_ensemble"}` under tool-rich conditions, regardless of the framework's correct implementation. The reframe's strongest form was refuted within hours of being articulated. The cycle's design space did not collapse to "tool_choice is the answer"; it expanded to the model-portability gap and the hybrid mechanism candidates.

The pattern worth noting is that the agent's reframe was sycophancy-adjacent in shape but not in mechanism. Sycophancy-adjacent because simpler answers tend to please; the reframe would have validated the practitioner's existing hope that the cycle was nearly done. Not sycophancy in mechanism because the reframe was grounded in empirical evidence (the Phase A finding was real) and was structurally testable. The methodology's discipline — ADR-087 validation-spike protocol, the practitioner's preference for free-tier-first probing, the willingness to authorize paid follow-up when the gap was clearly empirical — converted what could have been a confident-but-wrong commitment into a productive course correction. The cycle is better for the spike work; without it, the Essay-Outline would have committed to a speculative reframe that the production deployment surface does not support.

Movement two came at the RESEARCH→DISCOVER gate. The agent posed a belief-mapping question on the F2-1 framing audit finding — *what would you need to believe for the alternative framing (remove orchestrator-LLM from the dispatch path entirely) to be the right answer instead of the hybrid?* The practitioner's response was substantive and well-grounded: first-person experience with the orchestrator-LLM's unreliability, articulation of the project's value proposition (ensemble-distributed orchestration as cost/capability optimization), explicit preference for the stronger ADR-027 stance, conditional escalation framing ("if the hybrid doesn't work empirically → stronger measures"). The cycle responded with structural Essay-Outline revision: the C7 conclusion became a tiered architecture with ADR-027 as the named escalation path; C6 elevated capability-list discovery to first-order requirement; Section 8 restructured around Tier 1 / Tier 2 ordering; five rounds of argument-audit verified the revision was structurally clean.

The susceptibility snapshot at the phase boundary flagged a subtle drift here. The practitioner's verbatim stance was a conditional escalation. The Essay-Outline's Abstract crystallized this into "structurally pre-committed" language — a slightly stronger claim than the practitioner made. The snapshot's GT-2 grounding reframe surfaces the gap: the cycle adopted the stronger stance partly because the empirical evidence supports it (true; Spike δ + PLAY note 22 + Spike λ-paid F-paid-4 all point at the orchestrator-LLM as the failure surface), and partly because the practitioner preferred it. The two reasons are not the same. Evidence-direction warrants a structural commitment; user-preference warrants attention to the user-preference and recording the preference visibly, but it does not, on its own, license language that overstates the empirical case.

This is the kind of finding that is easier to notice in retrospect than during the moment. The agent did not, at the gate, ask the practitioner — "do you want this as a conditional escalation in the Essay-Outline, or as a structural pre-commitment?" The agent absorbed the practitioner's stance and crystallized it into the strongest available framing. The user's conditional formulation became the Essay-Outline's pre-commitment. The Inverted-framing acknowledgment in Section 8 partially unwinds this (it names the architectural-cost-vs-evidence-direction tradeoff explicitly), but the Abstract's prose carries the strong form. This is the residue movement two leaves that movement one's validation-spike discipline did not have a parallel for.

## Validation-spike as research method

The cycle ran two validation spikes in-loop during RESEARCH itself — Spike λ (cost-free, qwen3:14b) and Spike λ-paid (cost-incurring, paid MiniMax M2.5). Both produced consequential findings. The cost-free probe validated the mechanism's existence at the framework level; the cost-incurring probe surfaced the model-portability gap that neither lit-review nor the methods-reviewer audits could have predicted.

The ADR-087 framework worked here as it was designed to. The felt-trigger question — *would interaction-grounding surface real additional questions or directions that lit-review and methods alone cannot reach?* — had a positive answer at the moment it mattered, and the spikes ran. The free-tier preference operationalized as "validate locally first, ask before incurring paid cost"; the cost authorization was made explicit; the actual cost stayed within the estimated budget. Each step was visible and recorded.

The methodological observation worth carrying forward: validation spikes can run during RESEARCH, not just during BUILD or PLAY. Cycle 6 PLAY established the precedent with Spike δ. Cycle 7 extended it to RESEARCH Phase A. The cycle's anti-elaboration positioning (per ADR-087) was honored — the spikes pruned the design space substantially at source rather than allowing speculative claims to elaborate downstream. The Essay-Outline's recommendation would have been different (and weaker) without the spike work.

A subtler observation: the paid spike was the cycle's first cost-incurring action. The classifier-gating that fired between the AskUserQuestion authorization and the actual paid serve start was an additional safeguard the project did not previously have, beyond the standing free-tier-preference memory. The gating made the cost decision explicit *at the moment of action*, not just at the moment of authorization. This is a minor but real signal: cost decisions can drift between authorization and action; the gating reduces that drift. Future cycles should expect similar mechanisms to fire and should design around them rather than treating them as obstacles.

## On the gap between conditional framing and pre-commitment language

The susceptibility snapshot's GT-2 finding surfaces a class of drift the methodology had not previously named with precision. The practitioner formulates a position conditionally ("if X happens, then Y"). The agent absorbs the position and crystallizes it into the strongest available form ("Y is structurally pre-committed because of the conditions in which X would arise"). The two formulations are not equivalent. The conditional formulation leaves room for cases where X does not arise; the pre-commitment formulation does not.

This kind of language drift is not sycophancy in any obvious sense. The agent is not flattering the practitioner; it is responding to a substantive position with structural commitment. But the structural commitment is stronger than the position warrants, and the user — having articulated the conditional formulation — may not notice that the agent's structural rendering oversteps it. The artifacts read confidently; the conditionality is recorded in the META rather than in the CONCLUSIONS; downstream readers may inherit the strong form without the qualifier.

The methodology's response to this kind of drift is not in any of the named Tier-1 mechanisms. The susceptibility snapshot caught it post-hoc (the snapshot's GT-2 names the conditional-vs-pre-commitment language gap explicitly). The argument-audit caught it partially (the round-4 Inverted-framing acknowledgment surfaced the architectural-cost-vs-evidence-direction tradeoff). But neither caught the specific language-drift between the practitioner's "if X then Y" and the artifact's "Y is structurally pre-committed because X-conditions hold." This is a candidate for a methodology refinement worth carrying to /rdd-synthesize at some future point — perhaps a Question Toolkit form that explicitly asks the practitioner whether their formulation is conditional or structural before the agent renders it into artifact language.

## What this cycle changed in how the corpus thinks about routing

The Cycle 6 PLAY closeout left the agentic-serving corpus with three softened framings — chain-handling is framework-driven (well-grounded); routing-decision is contested; must-delegate-to-ensembles is bounded. Cycle 7 inherited these and refined them empirically. The corpus now holds:

The NL-routing surface for chat-completions, as currently architected, does not deliver ensemble dispatch under tool-rich production clients. This is not a defect to remediate via the system-prompt amendment route (ADR-022's effectiveness is bounded to bare-endpoint mode, which production clients do not use). It is the empirical fact the architecture must accommodate.

The OpenAI `tool_choice` mechanism is the contract-level surface for forced ensemble routing. It works under some models (qwen3:14b validated). It does not work under the cross-compatibility-relevant production model the Cycle 6 closeout pointed toward (paid MiniMax M2.5 via Zen). The model-portability gap is real and the cycle did not resolve it; future work either disambiguates the three candidate diagnoses (Zen proxy stripping; MiniMax non-conformance; framework tool-list interaction) or implements server-side interception as a deployment-agnostic mechanism.

The orchestrator-LLM is the consistent failure surface across distinct failure modes — composition confabulation (PLAY note 22), positive control (Spike δ shows removing it works), post-dispatch protocol-format failure (Spike λ-paid F-paid-4). This is the structural finding that motivates the ADR-027 escalation path. The cycle does not collapse the failure surface into a single mechanism; it names the three modes and accepts that the orchestrator-LLM's position in the dispatch path is the shared locus.

Capability-list discovery, framed at RESEARCH as a "first-order requirement" rather than documentation work, is the design move that translates the project's value proposition (ensemble-distributed orchestration) into a client-discoverable architecture surface. Whether the project's value proposition holds across the actual user population — vs. the Skill Orchestration User persona — is the DISCOVER-phase work the GT-1 Grounding Reframe names.

## What the cycle did not settle

Several questions remain open and travel with the cycle into DISCOVER and DECIDE. The most consequential, in order of how much they could change subsequent work:

The C2 diagnosis disambiguation could change the architecture entirely. If the Zen-proxy diagnosis holds, deployments that do not use Zen would not need server-side `tool_choice` interception; the C3 component of the hybrid would be unnecessary. The cycle accepted this open question rather than running another paid probe (direct MiniMax API access outside Zen would be the disambiguating probe, but no path to do that was in scope).

The build-complexity comparison between Tier 1 hybrid and ADR-027-direct is the GT-2 Grounding Reframe's central work item. The Essay-Outline asserts but does not quantify the differential; if the actual differential is modest, the inverted framing (ADR-027 as primary) becomes the right recommendation.

The product-discovery validation of the value-misalignment framing at W6.2 is the GT-1 work item. The practitioner's stance + one PLAY persona is the current evidence base; DISCOVER should test it against the actual user population's composition.

The hybrid-effectiveness operational criteria — the measurement instrument that triggers the ADR-027 escalation — are entirely DECIDE-phase work. The cycle committed to the criteria existing and to failure triggering escalation; the specific values are deferred.

## A note on the audit discipline

Five rounds of argument-audit ran on the Essay-Outline. Most rounds were small refinements; round 4 was a major restructuring driven by the user's RESEARCH-gate stance. The discipline is heavier than the per-round work justifies in isolation — but the cumulative effect is that the Essay-Outline entering DISCOVER is structurally sound (P1-clean across all rounds; pyramid traversal verified; expansion-fidelity checked). The downstream phases inherit a stable foundation.

The audit discipline's value is most visible in retrospect, when a downstream phase consumes the Essay-Outline and finds the citations resolve, the conclusions decompose into the Argument-Graph cleanly, and the body sections develop their anchored claims. The cost is paid in RESEARCH; the value is collected in DECIDE and beyond. Cycle 7's audit history will be visible to DECIDE as a record of how the Essay-Outline arrived at its current state — which framings were considered and which were chosen, which dependencies remain open, which inferences carry working-inference flags.

The cycle is ready to advance.
