# Cross-Compatibility Routing-Surface Architecture: Framework-Driven Dispatch Pipeline as Primary Direction

*2026-05-21; revised 2026-05-22 (DISCOVER + MODEL Amendment Log A1-A4 propagation; DECIDE OQ #18 refinement A2.1; AS-9 codification at MODEL boundary); amended 2026-05-24 (BUILD → RESEARCH loop-back: client-tool-action terminal — Amendment B; Spikes π/ρ/σ added C8)*

## Abstract

The cycle examined three linked architectural questions for the agentic-serving chat-completions surface: where the routing decision lives (Q1), how I/O contracts are enforced (Q2), and what fallback shape applies to non-capability-matched requests (Q3). Empirical grounding established that under production tool-rich clients, the NL-to-ensemble routing fraction is approximately zero — the orchestrator-LLM reliably routes to direct completion or client-tool delegation, not to ensemble dispatch (C1). The OpenAI-standard `tool_choice` parameter is **not** a free baseline mechanism: Spike κ source-code inspection established that the framework's `_ChatCompletionsRequest` Pydantic model has no `tool_choice` field and silently strips the parameter at the request-parsing boundary (D0 finding). The cross-compatibility-relevant production model (paid MiniMax M2.5 via OpenCode Zen) does not honor `tool_choice={"name":"invoke_ensemble"}` under tool-rich client conditions in any case — but the framework-portability gap is the load-bearing finding because it refutes the "free baseline" assumption and shifts the cost-equivalence calculus (C2). Per Cycle 7 RESEARCH GT-2(a) cost-equivalence rule and the build-complexity comparison produced at DECIDE entry, both candidate mechanisms require comparable new framework code; the **cycle's architectural recommendation is the ADR-027 framework-driven dispatch pipeline (plan → dispatch → synthesize, with the orchestrator-LLM removed from the routing-decision and post-dispatch-synthesis surfaces) as PRIMARY direction**, with the hybrid mechanism (server-side `tool_choice` interception layered onto NL inference) as a **conditional alternative** for operator-deployment shapes where some client population sends explicit `tool_choice` and expects forced ensemble routing. The recommendation is grounded in three converging signals: (a) **AS-9 codified at MODEL boundary** — structurally-bounded LLM roles produce reliable output on single-decision-shaped tasks where the orchestrator-LLM-as-decider failed, empirically grounded across four documented confabulation modes + 13 tests at qwen3:8b (Spike ε + ε' + μ); (b) **GT-2(a) cost-equivalence** established by Spike κ + the DECIDE-entry build-complexity comparison (`cycle-7-oq-19-build-complexity-comparison.md`); (c) **structural-bounding generalizes universally on the chat-completions surface** — ADR-027 reduces the NL-routing-fraction-to-ensemble-dispatch beyond the C1 baseline of approximately zero on every request, while the hybrid mitigates only the explicit-`tool_choice` subset. The Q3 fallback design honors the cost-distribution lens as architectural-layer concern, sharpened by OQ #18 Population A validation: the transparent-endpoint promise splits into (1) **configuration honesty** (Population A directly corroborates) and (2) **cost-distribution accountability** (Population A silent; project-developer-lens grounded); ADRs treat these as distinct commitments delivered by honest response labeling (1) and strict-dispatch-when-capability-matched (2). The Q2 I/O contract enforcement applies most consequentially at the synthesizer-agent layer, with tool-call-as-output-format as the path-agnostic primary mechanism for new ensembles and Calibration Gate Reflect as the lowest-cost retrofit for existing form drift. A BUILD → RESEARCH loop-back (2026-05-24) added C8: the pipeline as shipped (WP-A) terminates only in text and is single-turn, so it cannot deliver ensemble work to a tool-driven client's local filesystem — the north-star requirement. Spikes π/ρ/σ empirically establish that a **client-tool-action terminal** (emit OpenAI `tool_calls` the client executes) is necessary for parity, that framework-planner delegation composes with the terminal across a real OpenCode multi-turn loop, and that the integrated pattern (cheap loop-driver + per-turn ensemble delegation) holds end-to-end — surfacing two structural gaps ADR-027 lacks: a per-turn agentic-loop-driver (layer A) and an artifact-store → tool_call content bridge (F-ρ.1).

**CONCLUSIONS:**

- **C1.** Under empirically tested production tool-rich clients (n=1 family: OpenCode), NL framing routes the orchestrator-LLM to direct LLM completion or client-tool delegation but reliably not to ensemble dispatch; the NL-to-ensemble routing fraction is approximately zero under the production deployment surface as currently architected.
- **C2.** The OpenAI-standard `tool_choice` parameter is **not** a free baseline mechanism: Spike κ source-code inspection (D0 finding) established that the framework's `_ChatCompletionsRequest` Pydantic model has no `tool_choice` field and silently strips the parameter at the request-parsing boundary (`extra="ignore"`). The cross-compatibility-relevant production model (paid MiniMax M2.5 via OpenCode Zen) does not honor `tool_choice={"name":"invoke_ensemble"}` under tool-rich client conditions in any case — but the framework-portability gap is load-bearing because it refutes the "free baseline" assumption and shifts the cost-equivalence calculus toward ADR-027-direct per GT-2(a). Phase A's reframe (the existing `tool_choice` contract already addresses forced routing) is empirically invalidated; implementing `tool_choice` handling at any layer requires new framework code comparable in scope to ADR-027's pipeline implementation.
- **C3.** **The routing-planner ensemble is the PRIMARY mechanism** for the routing decision (Spike ζ — 100% JSON conformance + 100% schema validity + 90% strict capability-match across 20-prompt battery at qwen3:8b; cheap-tier-model produces conformant dispatch-plan JSON across diverse request shapes). Explicit-naming extractor and `tool_choice` interception layer onto NL inference as bonus paths, not as the load-bearing mechanism. The latency profile (~10s p50 at qwen3:8b) exceeds the R2-1 1.0s/20% bound but is a tuning concern not a structural blocker per DISCOVER 2026-05-21 framing (under Population A clients with permissive defaults; Cline's 30s hard default requires documented operator tuning per OQ #20); tuning axes (classifier pre-filter, cached planner decisions, smaller faster planner model, streaming synthesizer) are DECIDE-phase work.
- **C4.** The orchestrator-LLM's emergent composition pattern under forced dispatch — attempting to chain through a file-read of the dispatched ensemble's substrate path — fails at the production client interface because production clients cannot execute file reads against the server's filesystem, motivating framework-driven composition continuation that surfaces dispatch results directly as chat-completion content. Under ADR-027-direct, this surfaces as the response-synthesizer ensemble taking `(REQUEST + PLAN + DISPATCH RESULTS)` as structured input under strict-fidelity rules; under the hybrid conditional alternative, it surfaces as a one-turn synthesizer step applied when explicit-`tool_choice` interception fires.
- **C5.** I/O contract enforcement (Q2) applies most consequentially to the paths where ensembles actually run (routing-planner-driven dispatch, direct invoke, hybrid-extension dispatch); form-drift persistence at the synthesizer-agent layer is the empirical surface this enforcement targets, with cross-path requirements favoring ensemble-authoring mechanisms (tool-call-as-output-format) over dispatch-layer mechanisms. Two-track DECIDE recommendation: tool-call-as-output-format for new ensembles (authoring-time, path-agnostic); Calibration Gate Reflect for existing ensembles with form drift (lowest-cost retrofit reusing ADR-014 + ADR-018 infrastructure).
- **C6.** The "fallback" shape for non-capability-matched requests is empirically the current direct-completion behavior under NL framing on tool-rich production clients, **and this fallback is in tension with the project's value proposition at the cost-distribution architectural layer** (DISCOVER 2026-05-22 sharpening; OQ #18 Population A validation 2026-05-22). The transparent-endpoint promise splits into two distinct sub-promises that ADRs must keep separate: (1) **configuration honesty** — the response truthfully reports what served it (direct completion vs. ensemble dispatch); Population A voice corroborates this directly (Cline #10551 context-window divergence; OpenCode #20859 subagent substitution as Population A degradation signals); (2) **cost-distribution accountability** — the project-developer expectation that ensembles do dispatch on capability-matched requests; Population A voice is silent on this sub-promise (Population A clients locate cost-distribution inside the client, not at the endpoint); the framing remains project-developer-lens grounded as honest residual uncertainty. The design work delivers (1) via honest response labeling and (2) via strict-dispatch-when-capability-matched, with capability-list discovery first-order (so clients can construct the right `tool_choice` shape — not documentation work) and structured advisory for Population B as separate axis.
- **C7.** The cycle's architectural recommendation for DECIDE is **ADR-027 framework-driven dispatch pipeline as PRIMARY direction**, with the **hybrid mechanism as conditional alternative** for operator-deployment shapes where some client population sends explicit `tool_choice` and expects forced ensemble routing. The tier ordering of the pre-DISCOVER framing flips after Spike κ + ε + μ: Spike κ established cost-equivalence (both ADR-027-direct and Tier 1 hybrid require comparable new framework code; the "free baseline" assumption for `tool_choice` is refuted by D0; the DECIDE-entry build-complexity comparison estimates Tier 1 ~14 person-days and ADR-027-direct ~16 person-days, within ~30% spread); Spike ε empirically validated ADR-027's structural advantage on the routing-decision and post-dispatch-synthesis surfaces (confabulation pattern from PLAY note 22 dissolves when the orchestrator-LLM is replaced by a structurally-bounded synthesizer at the same cheap tier); Spike μ extended the empirical coverage of the structural-bounding finding across four documented confabulation modes + 13 tests at qwen3:8b. **AS-9 was codified at MODEL boundary (2026-05-22)** as the constitutional invariant capturing the structural property; AS-9 is satisfied by ADR-027 universally on the chat-completions surface and by the hybrid only on the explicit-`tool_choice` subset.
- **C8.** *(Added 2026-05-24 — BUILD → RESEARCH loop-back; see Amendment B.)* The Cycle 7 dispatch pipeline (ADR-027), as designed and as shipped in WP-A, terminates only in text (`ContentDelta` + `Completion`) and is single-turn; it cannot deliver ensemble-produced work to a tool-driven client's local filesystem — which the project's north-star (OpenCode-driven, ensemble-delegated, long-horizon coding) requires. A **client-tool-action terminal** is necessary: the pipeline must emit OpenAI `tool_calls` the client executes locally. The necessity rests on the client's **execution model** (the client drives and observes its filesystem through tool calls it executes), not on filesystem geography (which dissolves under co-location of `llm-orc serve` and the client). Spikes π/ρ/σ (2026-05-24; OpenCode 1.15.5 + local qwen3; $0) establish at the mechanism level: (π) direct co-located write + text acknowledgment fails parity — the client acts on an unverified claim, not an observed tool result; (ρ) framework-planner-driven delegation + the `tool_calls` terminal compose with parity, and the C1 tool-rich-client routing suppression does **not** recur under the bounded routing planner (observed on one capability-matched task; the structural basis is AS-10 — the planner routes on request content, not on the client's declared tools); (σ.1/σ.2) a cheap local model sustains the client's multi-turn agentic loop (grounded by a real tool result each turn), and the integrated pattern — cheap loop-driver + per-turn ensemble delegation — composes end-to-end, with an ensemble-generated test passing. Two structural consequences ADR-027 does not address: (a) a **per-turn agentic-loop-driver (layer A)** is a *role* no current ADR-027 component holds — neither the routing planner (which-capability, one-shot) nor the ensembles (generation) drive the loop; who fills the layer-A seat is an open architectural question for DECIDE/ARCHITECT, with three named candidates and one load-bearing grounded-loop hypothesis recorded in the C8 sub-tree (W8.6) — candidates, not findings; (b) the **artifact-store → tool_call bridge** (F-ρ.1): capability ensembles route deliverables to the server-side artifact store by design (ADR-025), so the terminal must read the artifact and marshal it into the client tool_call. ADR-027 is **structurally under-specified for the multi-turn agentic behavior the north-star requires, not merely missing a stage**; the fix aligns with its philosophy (deterministic framework wrapping is more reliable than the orchestrator-LLM that failed at PLAY note 22).

  - EVIDENCE: A full routing-planner ensemble would add 1-3s ensemble-dispatch latency under cheap-tier hardware (research-log §R2-1 estimate); an explicit-naming extractor adds ~0ms; a classifier adds ~50-200ms.
  - SYNTHESIS: The explicit-naming extractor is the cheapest mechanism but covers only the explicit-naming case. A hybrid (extractor for explicit naming + classifier or planner for residual) is the most likely architecture given the latency bound; the cycle's DECIDE phase should test the routing-planner against the bound on representative hardware.

  - EVIDENCE: ADR-025 establishes substrate routing; dispatch envelope carries `primary` and `artifacts[0]` summary-shaped content; substrate path written with `.retention` markers (adr-025; cycle-6-field-notes-play §note 8 operationally observed).

  - SYNTHESIS: The architectural pattern that emerged across Cycle 6 PLAY (substrate routing observed working) + Spike δ (framework-driven chaining works) + Spike λ-paid (orchestrator-LLM chaining via file-reads fails) is consistent: the framework should be the agent of composition continuation. The orchestrator-LLM is the chaining failure surface; the framework is the consistent success surface.

This is the path-independence finding.
  - EVIDENCE: Cycle 6 PLAY note 15 corroborates the cross-path observation on the orchestrator-LLM-chained path: same drift pattern; note 15 records the synthesis directly ("same drift as note 5... now observed in two configurations: direct invoke and chained via orchestrator. The spec drift is at the synthesizer/agent layer.") (cycle-6-field-notes-play §note 15).
  - SYNTHESIS: Form drift is the synthesizer agent's behavior. It is present across direct invoke, orchestrator-chained, and framework-chained paths. Q2's enforcement target is the synthesizer agent's output conformance, not the path that gets to it.

- CLAIM: Q2 options evaluated against the cross-path requirement favor ensemble-authoring mechanisms.
  - EVIDENCE: Q2 option (b) tool-call-as-output-format: ensemble's synthesizer agent calls a `submit_results` tool whose params ARE the schema. This is an authoring-time decision (YAML), path-agnostic (works on direct invoke + chat-completions + tool_choice-forced dispatch + framework-driven chain). (research-design-review-cycle-7 §Q2 Concern 5; research-log Step 1.4).
  - EVIDENCE: Q2 option (a) schema-as-enforcement with retry: dispatch-layer mechanism. Cross-path: requires retry state in the framework; direct-invoke path needs separate retry handling. (research-log Step 1.4).
  - EVIDENCE: Q2 option (d) Calibration Gate as enforcement: reuses existing Reflect verdict + retry infrastructure (ADR-014, ADR-018). Cross-path: applies within ensemble dispatches regardless of invocation path. (research-log Step 1.4).

For existing ensembles with form-drift (`claim-extractor`), option (d) is the lowest-cost intervention (uses existing infrastructure). - META-OBSERVATION: SCOPE QUALIFICATION on Q2: content drift (orchestrator-narration substitution per Spike γ Cell A-explicit, where the synthesizer's `chars.reverse()` was substituted with a hand-rolled loop in the orchestrator's narration) is downstream of the ensemble output boundary and is outside Q2's solution space. Framework-driven composition continuation (C4) addresses content drift by removing the orchestrator-narration step entirely for substrate-routed dispatches. Q2 targets form drift only.

### Section 7: Q3 fallback — empirical default in tension with project value; capability discovery is first-order (C6)

- CLAIM: The empirical "fallback" for non-capability-matched requests is the current direct-completion behavior under NL framing on tool-rich production clients.
  - EVIDENCE: C1's evidence base directly supplies this — see W1.1, W1.2 evidence.
  - WARRANT: The cycle's design space for Q3 is what to specify as the **contract** for this behavior, not what mechanism to add. The mechanism is already operational.

- CLAIM: The empirical fallback (direct LLM completion) is in tension with the project's value proposition: llm-orc users reach for the chat-completions endpoint because they want ensemble-distributed orchestration; if they wanted frontier-model direct completion, they would use the frontier model directly.
  - WARRANT: The project's stated identity centers on ensemble-distributed orchestration as a cost/capability optimization — smaller models composed declaratively to serve work that a frontier model would otherwise handle directly. Under this framing, every direct-completion response is a missed opportunity to deliver the project's value.
  - EVIDENCE: Practitioner stance recorded at the cycle 7 RESEARCH gate (2026-05-21): *"llm-orc's stance is that ensembles are used to distribute costs and token to distribute, declarative orchestrations of smaller models. So the orchestrator NEEDs to do that. Otherwise we'd simply use the frontier model. But if we're reaching for llm-orc we are already saying 'we need to optimize for local distribution' or at least 'targeted model' orchestration for tasks"* **(working inference grounded in practitioner stance; pending DECIDE-phase validation against product-discovery's stakeholder mental models).**
  - EVIDENCE: Cycle 6 PLAY corroborated the value-alignment expectation: Skill Orchestration User stakeholder's super-objective was to "Compose a skill framework against the orchestrator's capability library; expect dispatch when a capability slot fits" — empirical NL routing failed to honor this expectation [cycle-6-field-notes-play §Stakeholder: Skill Orchestration User].
  - SYNTHESIS: Q3 design therefore prioritizes mechanisms that REDUCE reliance on direct completion as the silent default, not a fallback shape that accepts direct completion as satisfactory.

- CLAIM: Capability-list discovery is a FIRST-ORDER requirement, not documentation work.
  - EVIDENCE: The chat-completions endpoint can advertise available ensembles via `/v1/models` extension, sibling endpoint, or response metadata; this is what makes ensemble routing discoverable to clients who want it (working inference from W6.3 + practitioner stance at cycle 7 RESEARCH gate).
  - EVIDENCE: Without capability-list discovery, clients constructing `tool_choice={"name":"<ensemble>"}` cannot know which ensemble names are valid; the OpenAI-standard tool_choice mechanism is structurally inert without discoverable capability identifiers (working inference from C3 + tool_choice mechanics per openai-fc-guide).

- CLAIM: The two caller populations (A: tool-call-aware client without alternative-surface access; B: developer/script client with alternative-surface access) differ in their fallback needs; structured advisory and operator-observable degradation signaling address them differently.
  - EVIDENCE: Population A receives the current direct-completion behavior — coherent NL response, transparent to the client; the design work is to make this a STRUCTURED degradation surface (metadata signals capability-list + tool_choice-recommendation), not the silent default (research-log Step 1.4 revised Q3 + cycle 7 RESEARCH gate stance).
  - EVIDENCE: Population B can be redirected to `llm-orc invoke` via a structured advisory in the response or via a structured error code (research-log Step 1.4 revised Q3).
  - EVIDENCE: Operator-observable degradation signaling — deployment dashboards / logs surface direct-completion-fallback rate as a degradation signal; high rates indicate clients are not finding the ensemble-routing path the project's value depends on **(working inference; this intersects ADR-023's event surface; pending DECIDE-phase routing decision).**

### Section 8: Cycle 7 recommended architecture and scope-of-claim (C7; META scope)

- SYNTHESIS: The cycle's architectural recommendation for the Cycle 7 DECIDE phase is a **tiered architecture** comprising a starting commitment (hybrid mechanism) AND a named escalation path (ADR-027 framework-driven dispatch pipeline). The hybrid is structurally preferred only insofar as it preserves more of the current architecture; the escalation is structurally pre-committed because the orchestrator-LLM is the recurring failure surface across the cycle's empirical evidence.

  **Tier 1 — Hybrid mechanism (starting commitment):**
  - (i') Server-side `tool_choice` interception via routing-planner ensemble OR explicit-naming extractor OR small classifier — chosen against the R2-1 latency bound on representative hardware. Delivers deterministic ensemble routing for clients that send standard OpenAI tool_choice values, independent of model honoring.
  - (ii') Framework-driven composition continuation — when any mechanism dispatches an ensemble, the framework consumes the result and surfaces it as chat-completion content (NL synthesis from the dispatch envelope's `primary` field, OR direct artifact reference), NOT as a tool_call for substrate-path file-reads.
  - (iii') Q2 enforcement as a two-track recommendation — tool-call-as-output-format for new ensembles (authoring-time); Calibration Gate Reflect for existing ensembles with form drift (lowest-cost retrofit).
  - (iv') Q3 fallback shape: capability-list discovery (first-order, makes ensemble routing client-discoverable) + structured advisory for Population B + operator-observable degradation signaling. Direct completion is the empirical fallback today but is in tension with the project's value proposition and must not be the silent default.

  **Tier 2 — ADR-027 framework-driven dispatch pipeline (named escalation path):**
  - (v') ADR-027 candidate (Cycle 6 PLAY closeout): a deterministic `plan → dispatch → synthesize` pipeline where the orchestrator-LLM is removed from the dispatch path entirely. The planner ensemble produces the dispatch sequence; the framework executes each step; a synthesizer (potentially a dedicated ensemble, NOT the orchestrator-LLM in a chat-completion synthesis turn) produces the final client response from the dispatch results.
  - **Escalation trigger:** hybrid-effectiveness measurement during BUILD/PLAY against operational criteria (specific values DECIDE-phase work; candidate criteria recorded at the cycle 7 RESEARCH gate include routing reliability rate, composition fidelity rate, end-to-end NL routing rate vs. Phase A baseline, operator-observable failure rate). If the hybrid does not deliver effectiveness on the cross-compatibility surface, the cycle's commitment is to the stronger framework-driven dispatch pipeline rather than incrementally patching the hybrid.
  - **Why this is structurally pre-committed rather than a future-cycle option:** the empirical evidence pattern (Cycle 6 PLAY composition confabulation; Spike δ framework-driven success; Spike λ-paid orchestrator-LLM substrate-path failure mode) is consistent across **three distinct failure modes** — composition confabulation (multi-dispatch fabrication; PLAY note 22), composition success when the orchestrator-LLM is removed (Spike δ positive control), and post-dispatch protocol-format failure (Spike λ-paid F-paid-4 substrate-path file-read attempts in XML or as unreachable client-tool calls). The orchestrator-LLM is the **consistent failure surface across distinct failure modes**, not a single failure mode observed three times. The cycle accepts that the hybrid is a stepping stone whose justification rests on preserving the current architecture; the escalation path is the recognition that the recurring failure surface (the orchestrator-LLM in the dispatch path) may require structural removal rather than route-around mechanisms.

  **Inverted-framing acknowledgment.** The ordering rationale (hybrid first, ADR-027 as escalation) is grounded in architectural-continuity cost, not in evidence-direction. The evidence base (Spike δ + PLAY note 22 + Spike λ-paid F-paid-4) directly supports the ADR-027 path; the hybrid is preferred only insofar as architectural cost is the relevant comparison. Teams or deployments with lower architectural-continuity constraints should consider implementing ADR-027 directly, using Spike δ's framework-driven chaining as the starting implementation, rather than building the hybrid as an intermediate step. The Essay-Outline asserts but does not quantify the architectural-cost differential; DECIDE should weigh the comparison explicitly.

- SCOPE QUALIFICATION: The recommendation is empirically grounded for the OpenCode + MiniMax M2.5 + qwen3:14b axis the cycle probed. The model-portability claim (C2) is established for paid MiniMax M2.5 via OpenCode Zen. Other production model-tier-cloud-orchestrator profiles (Groq Llama-3, Cerebras, Anthropic Sonnet, OpenAI gpt-4o-mini, etc.) have not been probed. The DECIDE phase should treat the hybrid recommendation as conditional on representative model-portability characterization across deployment targets, AND must specify the hybrid-effectiveness operational criteria that trigger the ADR-027 escalation.

- SCOPE QUALIFICATION (empirical-grounding dependencies): The cycle's architectural recommendation rests on four working-inference evidence nodes in load-bearing warrant positions: **E3.1.1** (server-side `tool_choice` interception preserves client-facing API contract — pending validation against representative client-server integration test); **E4.2.1** (production clients' filesystem scope is disjoint from agentic-serving deployment's filesystem — pending validation against client-side filesystem-scope characterization); **E5.3.3** (framework-driven composition continuation eliminates the orchestrator-narration substitution surface — pending validation that the framework-driven path preserves content fidelity end-to-end under all dispatched ensemble types); **E6.2.1** (the project's value proposition centers on ensemble-distributed orchestration such that direct-completion fallback is a value misalignment — pending DECIDE-phase validation against product-discovery's stakeholder mental models). The accumulated epistemic risk is higher than any single inference node suggests. The DECIDE phase should treat this four-node cluster as the empirical-grounding dependency that gates the cycle's architectural commitments; each inference needs either independent corroboration or explicit acceptance of the residual uncertainty before BUILD work proceeds.

- META-OBSERVATION: The cycle's research journey illustrates the ADR-082 question-isolation protocol's value: the constraint-removal step (Step 1.2) surfaced the direct-`invoke` alternate path which the original question framing did not foreground; the two reviewer rounds caught artifact-derived option enumerations and embedded conclusions; Phase A's empirical work + Spike λ + Spike λ-paid produced a richer architectural answer than either the original framing or the post-Phase-A reframe predicted. The tiered framing (hybrid + ADR-027 escalation) emerged from the cycle 7 RESEARCH gate's belief-mapping exchange (2026-05-21) on F2-1's "remove orchestrator-LLM from dispatch path entirely" alternative — surfacing that the cycle's empirical pattern more strongly supported ADR-027 than the Essay-Outline's pre-gate framing had acknowledged. The tiered architecture preserves the hybrid's lower architectural cost while making the cycle's escalation commitment structurally visible.

- VALIDATION-SPIKE DECISION (ADR-087): Two validation spikes ran in-loop (Spike λ qwen3:14b; Spike λ-paid MiniMax M2.5). Both produced consequential findings — Spike λ confirmed the mechanism's existence; Spike λ-paid exposed the model-portability gap. The cycle's prototyping investment (~$0.05-0.30 cost-incurring + ~30min wall-clock) pruned the design space substantially (the reframe's collapse to "tool_choice is the answer" was refuted; the tiered hybrid+ADR-027 architecture was surfaced). Recording: validation spikes were the right investment per ADR-087's anti-elaboration positioning — without them, the Essay-Outline would have committed to a speculative reframe that the production model does not support.

### Section 9: The client-tool-action terminal (C8)

- CLAIM: ADR-027's pipeline as shipped (WP-A: commits `e538264` + `0a7a822`) terminates only in text (`ContentDelta` + `Completion`) and is single-turn; it cannot deliver ensemble-produced work to a tool-driven client's local filesystem, which the north-star (OpenCode invoking work delegated to ensembles, files written locally) requires.
  - WARRANT: A surface that never emits `finish_reason: "tool_calls"` gives the client nothing to execute; ensemble deliverables land server-side and the client cannot reach them.
  - EVIDENCE: WP-A handler swap removed the `ClientToolCall` emission path; the pipeline emits text only (agentic-serving-cycle-status §"BUILD-surfaced finding: client-tool-action terminal gap").
  - SYNTHESIS: ADR-027 built the answer-a-question terminal, not the do-work-on-the-client terminal. Incomplete, not wrong.

- CLAIM: A `tool_calls` round-trip is necessary for parity; co-located direct write is not sufficient.
  - EVIDENCE: Spike π Phase A — server wrote `hello.py` directly and returned a text ack; OpenCode emitted one `text` event, zero tool events; it executed nothing and acted on an unverified claim (research-log-loopback §Spike π Phase A).
  - EVIDENCE: Spike π Phase B — server emitted a streamed `write` tool-call; OpenCode executed it, fed back "Wrote file successfully", and closed the loop with parity (research-log-loopback §Spike π Phase B).
  - SYNTHESIS: Parity rests on the client's execution model, not filesystem geography — co-location of `llm-orc serve` and the client dissolves the geography argument (E4.2.1) but the conclusion survives.

- CLAIM: The loop-back's original justification is corrected — the terminal is required by the client's *execution model*, not filesystem *geography*. This is C8's key epistemic move (it overturns the premise the loop-back finding leaned on).
  - WARRANT: Co-locating `llm-orc serve` with the client makes direct local write feasible, dissolving the disjoint-filesystem premise (E4.2.1). Yet parity still requires `tool_calls`, because the client drives and observes its filesystem through tool calls it executes — a property co-location does not change. The geography argument is contingent; the execution-model argument is not.
  - EVIDENCE: Practitioner constraint-removal at re-entry (ADR-082) separated *delivery* (trivial under co-location) from *parity* (requires client-executed tool calls) (research-log-loopback §Step 1.2 constraint-removal).
  - EVIDENCE: Spike π Phase A achieved delivery (file on disk) without parity (no client execution), demonstrating the two are distinct and that delivery alone does not give parity (research-log-loopback §Spike π Phase A).
  - SYNTHESIS: C8 survives its strongest counter-framing (co-location), because the load-bearing reason for the terminal is execution-model, not geography. A reader who accepts co-location must still accept the terminal.

- CLAIM: Framework-planner delegation, the `tool_calls` terminal, and a multi-turn agentic loop compose end-to-end through a real OpenCode session.
  - EVIDENCE: Spike ρ — the routing planner decided `dispatch → code-generator` on the real tool-rich request (delegation framework-decided, not hardcoded); the loop closed with parity; C1 tool-rich-client suppression did not recur (research-log-loopback §Spike ρ).
  - EVIDENCE: Spike σ.1 — qwen3:14b (free local) drove a 3-step task to completion, grounded each turn by a real tool result; the test ran and passed (research-log-loopback §Spike σ.1).
  - EVIDENCE: Spike σ.2 — the cheap loop-driver decided the trajectory while `write` content was delegated to the ensemble; the ensemble-generated test passed (research-log-loopback §Spike σ.2).
  - SYNTHESIS: The north-star pattern — orchestrator drives the loop, ensembles do the work — composes with parity; the grounded loop succeeds where note-22's ungrounded composition failed.

- CLAIM: Two structural gaps ADR-027 lacks are now named — and layer A in particular is a *role*, not a stage. ADR-027 is structurally under-specified for the multi-turn agentic behavior the north-star requires; the fix is not a bolt-on terminal.
  - EVIDENCE: Layer A (per-turn agentic-loop-driver) is a distinct *role* — neither the routing planner (which-capability, one-shot) nor the ensembles (generation) hold it; σ confirms layer A is required and is fillable by a local model on a short grounded task (qwen3:14b drove OpenCode's loop end-to-end in σ.1) (research-log-loopback §Spike σ, layer-A/B distinction).
  - EVIDENCE: **Three candidate seat-fillers for layer A:** (1) a model in the OpenAI "model" seat (σ-tested with qwen3:14b); (2) the routing planner (ADR-028) extended from one-shot router to per-turn decider; (3) a new dedicated layer-A component distinct from planner and synthesizer. DECIDE/ARCHITECT select; the candidates are *candidates*, not findings.
  - EVIDENCE: **Grounded-loop hypothesis** — the load-bearing discriminating question for candidate (1); *working inference, explicitly NOT a spike finding*: if σ.1's success and note-22's failure reflect a structural grounded-vs-ungrounded property (real tool result every turn vs. self-narrated multi-dispatch), candidate (1) is consistent with AS-9 (grounded role ≠ the ungrounded role AS-9 removed). If the hypothesis fails, AS-9's scope reopens specifically around the grounded-vs-ungrounded framing (not the bounded-role pattern generally), and the seat tilts toward (2) or (3).
  - EVIDENCE: **Why no further stand-in spiking is warranted before DECIDE/ARCHITECT.** The empirical contrast σ.1-vs-note-22 already supplies the grounded-vs-ungrounded contrast at the level a stand-in spike could provide. The discriminating evidence the hypothesis actually needs is a long-horizon multi-turn run (20+ turns, harder task) against the *real* built terminal with a real ensemble in the layer-B seat — a BUILD-phase validation target, not a research-phase spike. Running another stand-in would mostly repeat what π/ρ/σ already establish.
  - EVIDENCE: F-ρ.1 artifact bridge — capability ensembles route deliverables to the server-side artifact store by design (`output_substrate: artifact`, ADR-025); the terminal must read the artifact and marshal it into the client tool-call (research-log-loopback §Spike ρ, finding F-ρ.1).

- SCOPE QUALIFICATION: C8's findings are mechanism-level, n=1 tasks, OpenCode 1.15.5 + local qwen3. Not established: sustained long-horizon driving (dozens of turns, harder tasks, latency compounding), end-to-end through the *production* `code-generator` (its artifact substrate per F-ρ.1 is the BUILD-phase concern), `edit`/`bash`/multi-file coverage, direct-fallback on non-matched requests, and interactive-mode parity (all runs used headless `opencode run`; permission prompts and diff/undo UX are untested). OpenCode also declares native `skill` and `task` (subagent) tools (Phase 0 observation) — the surface a future "run RDD via OpenCode" flow would use; noted for ARCHITECT, not tested here. These are BUILD-phase validation targets, better tested against the real built terminal than a stand-in. (CONFIDENCE-LEVEL: mechanism viability empirically established; long-horizon robustness untested.)

**Trigger.** Cycle 7 reached BUILD WP-A (handler swapped to the ADR-027 dispatch pipeline; commits `e538264` + `0a7a822`). A BUILD reflection-gate surfaced that the pipeline emits text only and is single-turn — it never returns `finish_reason: "tool_calls"`, so it cannot deliver ensemble work to a tool-driven client's local filesystem, which the practitioner's north-star (OpenCode invoking work delegated to ensembles, files written locally) requires. The cycle looped back to RESEARCH to design and empirically validate the missing terminal (agentic-serving-cycle-status §"BUILD-surfaced finding" + §"Path forward").

**Question-isolation (ADR-082) on re-entry.** The constraint-removal step bracketed ADR-027's pipeline and surfaced (practitioner) that co-locating `llm-orc serve` with the client makes direct local write feasible — separating *delivery* (bytes to disk; trivial under co-location) from *parity* (the client behaving as a normal agentic session). The methods-reviewer (`research-design-review-cycle-7-loopback.md`) flagged that the first-draft research question embedded `tool_calls` as the answer; the question was reformulated to test necessity (direct-write vs. `tool_calls`) rather than presuppose it.

**Spike findings (π/ρ/σ; 2026-05-24; OpenCode 1.15.5 + local qwen3; $0 — all in [research-log-loopback]):**
- **π** — `tool_calls` is necessary for parity. Direct co-located write + text ack lands the file but the client executes nothing and acts on an unverified claim (PLAY-note-22 trust surface); the `tool_calls` round-trip gives full multi-turn parity.
- **ρ** — framework-planner delegation + the terminal compose; the C1 tool-rich-client routing suppression does not recur under the bounded routing planner (AS-10).
- **σ.1 / σ.2** — a cheap local model sustains OpenCode's multi-turn loop (grounded each turn by a real tool result), and the integrated pattern (cheap loop-driver + per-turn ensemble delegation) composes end-to-end, with an ensemble-generated test passing.

**Two structural findings carried to DECIDE/ARCHITECT/BUILD:**
- **Layer A as a role, not a stage.** A per-turn agentic-loop-driver (layer A) is a *role* no current ADR-027 component holds — the routing planner decides which-capability (one-shot); the ensembles generate content. Required and fillable by a local model on a short grounded task (σ used qwen3:14b directly as the seat-filler). **Who fills the seat is an open architectural question** for DECIDE/ARCHITECT, with three named candidates: (1) a model in the OpenAI "model" seat (σ-tested); (2) the routing planner extended from one-shot router to per-turn driving; (3) a new dedicated layer-A component. The **grounded-loop hypothesis** — that σ.1's success and note-22's failure reflect a structural grounded-vs-ungrounded property — is the load-bearing discriminating question for candidate (1)'s consistency with AS-9; it is a working inference, **NOT** a spike finding. Discriminating evidence belongs to BUILD-phase long-horizon validation against the real built terminal, not another stand-in spike.
- **F-ρ.1 — artifact-store → tool_call bridge.** Capability ensembles route deliverables to the server-side artifact store by design (`output_substrate: artifact`, ADR-025); the terminal must read the artifact and marshal it into the client tool-call. The designed-in form of the disjoint-filesystem problem; persists even under co-location.
- **F-ρ.2 — RETRACTED (verification error; caught by the citation re-audit).** An earlier draft claimed `agentic-tier-cheap-general` was undefined. It is defined at `.llm-orc/profiles/agentic-tier-cheap-general.yaml` (→ `qwen3:8b`/ollama, `cost_per_token: 0.0`, since Cycle 5 / ADR-015). The error came from relying on `llm-orc list-profiles`, which does not enumerate the `.llm-orc/profiles/` directory. No config-hygiene blocker; the production `code-generator` resolves its cheap tier on the free tier. Retained as a recorded correction (not deleted) per the cycle's audit-trail discipline.
