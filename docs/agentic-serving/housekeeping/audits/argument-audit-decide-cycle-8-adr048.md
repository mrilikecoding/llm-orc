### P1 — Must Fix

**P1-1. The ADR's Provenance check and Conditional-Acceptance section both list "independence-via-isolation" as spike-grounded, accepted architecture — but the spike's own method (four static fixtures, no live builder in the loop) tests discrimination, not independence. Independence rests entirely on an untested architectural-isolation argument, and the argument has an unexamined hole: the isolation boundary explicitly still passes the produced artifact to the judge.**

- **Location:** ADR-048 §Context (lines 19-24): "a minimal gate... **discriminated** correct / trivially-tested / wrong-code / false-adequate build outputs... No falsifier fired." Compare §Decision-2 (lines 43-49): "**Independence comes from architectural isolation plus determinism**... The builder controls neither the executor nor the judge. Independence is a property of seat isolation... not of an external process." Compare §Conditional Acceptance (lines 127-129): "The **architecture is accepted and spike-grounded**: the composed gate, **independence-via-isolation**, the deterministic-primary signal ordering, and acceptance-criteria-as-contract." Compare §Provenance check (line 137): "**Driver-derived:** the composed-gate shape and **independence-via-isolation follow from the Q2 grounding spike** and PLAY signal #3."
- **Claim:** Independence-via-isolation is a spike-grounded, accepted element of the architecture (not merely a design commitment awaiting validation).
- **Evidence gap:** The spike log's own Method section (lines 47-54) is explicit: the four fixtures (`a_correct`, `b_gamed_trivial_tests`, `c_wrong_code`, `d_false_adequate`) are hand-authored, static code+test pairs fed directly into the gate. There is no live builder seat in the loop attempting to produce output, observe the gate's behavior, or adapt to it — the "builder" is entirely simulated by pre-written fixture files. What the spike actually demonstrates is **discrimination**: given four fixed artifact states, the executor+judge combination correctly classifies each. That is a real and useful finding, and the Context section states it honestly ("discriminated... build outputs"). But the **independence** claim — that "the builder controls neither the executor nor the judge" — requires evidence that a *live* builder, present in the same session/ensemble, cannot influence the verifiers' behavior (via correlated model weights if builder and judge share a base model, via adaptive gaming across retries, or via content smuggled through the one channel the isolation boundary does not close). None of the pre-registered falsification criteria (F1: new primitive needed; F2: judge discrimination quality; F3: executor wiring) test this. The spike's own "Isolation boundary" section (lines 32-35) *asserts* "architectural isolation... is the source of independence" as part of its **Setup** (what was **built**), not as a **Result** the Method section tested — the spike's own text conflates a design choice with an empirical finding, and ADR-048's Provenance check inherits that conflation by citing "independence-via-isolation" as something that "follow[s] from the Q2 grounding spike." Separately, the isolation argument itself has a concrete gap the ADR does not examine: the boundary excludes "builder context or reasoning" (§Decision-2) but explicitly **includes** "the produced artifact" as one of the judge's three inputs (`{requirement, produced artifact, execution result}`). The produced artifact is exactly the one channel the builder fully controls — a builder (adversarial, misaligned, or simply optimizing against an observed rejection) could embed judge-directed content in code comments, docstrings, or output framing designed to influence the adequacy verdict. The spike's fixtures were benign (not adversarially crafted to test this channel), so this vector is untested, not merely under-tested.
- **Recommendation:** Reword the Context, Decision-2, and Provenance-check language to distinguish what the spike established (discrimination on static fixtures, ensemble-over-frontier feasibility) from what remains a design commitment (that seat isolation, as built, yields independence against a live, non-cooperative builder). Move "independence-via-isolation" out of "accepted and spike-grounded" in the Conditional-Acceptance section and into "Validation pending at BUILD," alongside a named validation target: whether the produced-artifact channel can carry builder-authored influence into the judge's verdict, and whether builder/judge model-weight correlation (if the same base model fills both roles) produces correlated rather than independent errors.
- **Routing:** N/A.

**P1-2. §Decision-3 demotes ADR-014's AUQ/HTC trajectory-confidence signal to "advisory only... it cannot be the gate" by assertion, not argument — and the assertion is inconsistent with the ADR's own inclusion logic (imperfect-but-complementary signals are anchored into the gate, not excluded from it), applied without acknowledgment to a signal ADR-046 explicitly described in exactly those complementary terms.**

- **Location:** ADR-048 §Decision-3 (lines 51-61): "2. **Isolated judges**... Independent via fresh context; **correlated-error-prone, so anchored by (1) wherever possible.** ... 4. **Trajectory confidence** (log-probability entropy, ADR-014 AUQ/HTC) — **advisory only**. It is builder-internal and blind to confident-wrong; it can flag uncertainty, it cannot be the gate." Compare ADR-046 §3 row `014` (source material, required reading): "ADR-014's **process-level AUQ/HTC signal** (verbalized confidence + generation-trajectory features: token-entropy, attention-weight distribution) is a **different signal type** — it **catches contract-conforming-but-anomalous generations a deterministic output check cannot see**... It is **not assumed subsumed** by the seat contract; it is a named **candidate for Q2 grounded acceptance**." Compare ADR-046 §Open: "contract assertions are a large part; **whether more is needed is a PLAY/BUILD validation target**."
- **Claim:** Trajectory confidence is categorically excluded from the gate (pure advisory), while the isolated judge — also imperfect (correlated-error-prone) — is included in the gate (anchored by deterministic execution).
- **Evidence gap:** ADR-048 applies one standard to tier 2 (imperfect but complementary → anchor and include) and a different, stricter standard to tier 4 (imperfect → exclude entirely), without arguing why the two imperfections warrant different treatment. The parallel is not incidental: ADR-046 described AUQ/HTC in precisely the "catches something the others structurally cannot see" terms that ADR-048's own Decision-1 uses to justify including the judge despite its imperfection ("the judge catches trivially-tested or under-covering outputs the executor passes... neither alone suffices"). If "catches a failure mode the other signals cannot see" is sufficient warrant to anchor-and-include a correlated-error-prone judge, ADR-048 owes an explicit argument for why the same warrant does not extend — even in anchored, non-primary form — to a signal ADR-046 already named as catching "contract-conforming-but-anomalous generations a deterministic output check cannot see." Instead, ADR-048 settles the question by assertion ("it cannot be the gate"), which also quietly forecloses the specific validation question ADR-046 recorded as open ("whether it's needed on top of contract assertions is a PLAY/BUILD validation target") — without citing ADR-046's framing, addressing it, or noting that a validation target is being closed by reasoning rather than by the evidence ADR-046 anticipated.
- **Recommendation:** Either (a) supply the missing argument for why "blind to confident-wrong" is categorically worse than "correlated-error-prone" (e.g., because confident-wrong failures are silent while correlated-error failures are at least sometimes independent of the executor's failure mode) and note this closes ADR-046's open validation question by reasoning, or (b) revise Decision-3 to place trajectory confidence as an anchored, non-primary tier-4 signal (parallel to the judge's tier-2 treatment) pending its own BUILD-phase calibration, matching how the ADR already treats the judge's unproven reliability.
- **Routing:** N/A.

**P1-3. ADR-048 does not state how its composed verification gate (executor/judge/gate seats) relates to ADR-046's seat-contract mechanism (`core/validation/`) — the two ADRs each describe an "independent correctness signal" for the same accept/another-round decision without reconciling whether they compose, one replaces the other, or they operate at different granularities.**

- **Location:** ADR-048 §Decision-1 (lines 34-41): "Grounded acceptance = deterministic execution AND an isolated contract-confidence judge, combined by a deterministic gate seat." Compare ADR-046 §2 (source material, required reading): "**The seat contract is the existing `core/validation/` framework**... This grounds Q2 / §6.2b: **the contract assertions are the independent correctness signal the loop's accept decision needed.**" Compare ADR-046 §Open: "Grounded acceptance beyond contract assertions (§6.2b / Q2): the accept/another-round decision's full independent signal (**contract assertions are a large part**; whether more is needed is a PLAY/BUILD validation target)."
- **Claim (implicit):** ADR-048's executor+judge+gate pipeline is the answer to "what independent signal does the accept decision rest on" (§Context: "the load-bearing requirement is that the accept signal be independent of the builder").
- **Evidence gap:** ADR-046 already named "contract assertions" (the `core/validation/` seat-contract framework: structural/schema/behavioral/quantitative/semantic layers, seat-owned, black-box, deterministic-first) as "the independent correctness signal the loop's accept decision needed," and framed Q2 as the question of "whether **more** is needed **on top of** contract assertions." ADR-048 never mentions `core/validation/`, `ValidationConfig`, `ValidationEvaluator`, or "seat contract" anywhere in its text. Per the spike log, the built gate is a plain script-node executor + LLM-node judge + script-node gate — architecturally distinct from the `core/validation/` framework ADR-046 designed. This leaves three live, unaddressed possibilities: (a) ADR-048's gate *is* "the more" ADR-046's Open item anticipated, composing on top of seat-contract admission (in which case the composition point needs stating — does a candidate first clear the seat contract, then face this gate?); (b) ADR-048's gate *replaces* the seat-contract as the accept mechanism (in which case ADR-046's seat-contract work is superseded, which should be said explicitly and is not); or (c) the two are answers to different questions entirely (seat admission vs. accept/another-round) that happen to overlap in vocabulary ("independent correctness signal," "gate") without overlapping in mechanism. A reader — and a BUILD-phase implementer — cannot tell which from ADR-048's text, and given ADR-046 §Open's seat-contract wiring is itself still "designed but unwired and unvalidated," the risk is two independently-specified, unreconciled accept mechanisms landing in the corpus.
- **Recommendation:** Add a paragraph (in Context or Decision-1) stating explicitly how the composed verification gate relates to the seat-contract mechanism ADR-046 designed — composes with it, supersedes it, or operates at a different point in the pipeline — so BUILD does not have to infer the relationship or accidentally build both as parallel, unreconciled accept paths.

### P2 — Should Fix

**P2-1. The orthogonal-complementarity claim (§Decision-1) and the coverage-aware-judge "surprise" (§Decision-5) rest on a thinner evidence base than the Decision prose implies: four fixtures split across two distinct toy tasks, with the coverage-awareness finding coming from a single fixture explicitly labeled `(probe)` — i.e., outside the pre-registered falsification design — rather than from the core F1–F3 test.**

- **Location:** ADR-048 §Decision-1 (line 38): "The spike's load-bearing result is that these catch orthogonal failures." §Decision-5 (lines 74-76): "the spike's judge caught a coverage gap only because the requirement stated the rule it missed." Compare spike log §Method (fixture table, lines 47-54): fixtures `a_correct`/`b_gamed_trivial_tests`/`c_wrong_code` share one small task ("missing `+32`"); `d_false_adequate` is a **different** task ("leap-year, omits century rule") and is explicitly marked `(probe)` in the table — distinguishing it from the pre-registered falsification fixtures. Compare §Pre-registered falsification (lines 39-42): F1/F2/F3 correspond to a/b/c; there is no pre-registered falsifier for the coverage-awareness finding.
- **Claim:** "No falsifier fired" and "the spike's load-bearing result" are presented uniformly across all four fixtures, including the coverage-awareness "surprise."
- **Evidence gap:** The core falsification test (F1–F3, the ADR's confident "no falsifier fired" claim) covers 3 fixtures on 1 toy task. The coverage-awareness finding driving §Decision-5 and the Positive-Consequences claim ("an isolated 8B judge did requirement-vs-test coverage analysis") comes from one additional, non-pre-registered probe fixture on a second, unrelated toy task. Probe/exploratory findings carry different evidentiary weight than pre-registered falsification results; ADR-048 doesn't distinguish the two when characterizing "the spike's load-bearing result," which reads as more uniformly tested than the design actually was.
- **Recommendation:** In Decision-1 and Decision-5, note that the coverage-awareness finding is a single exploratory probe on a second task, not a pre-registered falsification result, so the reader can weight it accordingly relative to F1–F3.
- **Routing:** N/A.

**P2-2. §Decision-5 and the Positive-Consequences bullet state the judge's coverage-awareness as an established design principle ("discriminating power scales with...") with more confidence than the spike's own hedged language, and the single-sample qualifier that does exist is placed in Negative Consequences rather than alongside the claim itself.**

- **Location:** ADR-048 §Decision-5 (line 74): "The judge's discriminating power **scales with** how specifically the requirement / acceptance criteria enumerate the cases that matter." §Consequences, Positive (line 112): "Concrete ensemble-over-frontier evidence: an isolated 8B judge **did** requirement-vs-test coverage analysis." Compare §Consequences, Negative (line 117): "The 8B judge's reliability is a **single-sample feasibility result**." Compare spike log §Caveats (lines 103-106): "Judge power **scales with** requirement specificity... A vaguer requirement **might not** prime the judge to demand that coverage" (hedged, conditional language).
- **Claim:** The scaling relationship between requirement specificity and judge coverage-detection is established.
- **Evidence gap:** The spike's own caveat uses "might not" — a hypothesis about an untested comparison case (no fixture varied requirement specificity while holding the bug constant to actually test the "scales with" relationship; fixture `d` tested one specific, fully-explicit requirement wording only). ADR-048's Decision-5 hardens this into an unqualified "scales with," and the Positive-Consequences bullet states the judge "did requirement-vs-test coverage analysis" as settled fact, while the qualifying "single-sample feasibility result" hedge sits in a different subsection (Negative). This is the same hedge-placement pattern flagged in the ADR-046 R1 audit (P2-3): the confident claim and its qualifier are textually separated.
- **Recommendation:** Add the single-sample qualifier inline where the coverage-awareness claim is first made (Decision-5), not only in Negative Consequences.
- **Routing:** N/A.

**P2-3. The parenthetical "(log-probability entropy, ADR-014 AUQ/HTC)" in §Decision-3 reduces ADR-014's multi-component signal to a single feature, under-crediting the mechanism being demoted.**

- **Location:** ADR-048 §Decision-3 (line 60): "**Trajectory confidence** (log-probability entropy, ADR-014 AUQ/HTC) — advisory only." Compare ADR-014 §Decision (lines 33-43, required reading per dispatch brief): the quality signal composition is **verbalized confidence** (AUQ, System 1 soft attention-propagation + System 2 binary threshold) **plus** HTC's **trajectory features** (token-level entropy patterns, **attention-weight distributions over tool-call sequences**, **decision-confidence trajectories across reasoning steps**), plus a defined **Abstain** criterion for **entropy collapse** (>1.5 SD drop from trajectory mean) — a richer, multi-signal composition than "log-probability entropy" names.
- **Claim:** AUQ/HTC is characterized, in parenthetical shorthand, as "log-probability entropy."
- **Evidence gap:** Log-probability entropy is one HTC feature among several (entropy patterns, attention-weight distributions, decision-confidence trajectories); AUQ's verbalized-confidence dual-process structure is a distinct, separately-motivated component not captured by "log-probability entropy" at all. The reduction makes the "blind to confident-wrong" claim read as more self-evidently true than it may be for the fuller signal (e.g., ADR-014's entropy-collapse Abstain criterion is specifically designed to catch trajectory anomalies that a purely output-level check would miss — whether it also catches confident-wrong cases is an empirical question the reduction sidesteps rather than answers).
- **Recommendation:** Either use ADR-014's own vocabulary (verbalized confidence + trajectory features) or, if "log-probability entropy" is meant as a representative shorthand, say so explicitly rather than presenting it as the mechanism's full content.
- **Routing:** N/A.

**P2-4. "False-adequate" is used to name two different, in fact opposite-outcome, things: the spike's caught fixture (a coverage-gap success) and the un-spikeable ceiling case (a permanent, uncaught gap) — the same term spans a demonstrated success and an argued-permanent failure mode.**

- **Location:** ADR-048 §Context (line 23): "discriminated correct / trivially-tested / wrong-code / **false-adequate** build outputs" (mirrors the spike's caught fixture `d`, a success). Compare §Decision-4 (line 68): "the held-out / property-based / golden oracle for the **false-adequate ceiling** (bugs on inputs the requirement never states — un-catchable by inspection by construction)" and §Consequences, Negative (line 116): "**The false-adequate ceiling stands** until the oracle rung is built." Compare spike log's own four-layer breakdown (lines 92-99): layer 3 ("buggy code, tests miss a **requirement-stated** case → coverage-aware judge") is the **caught** case (fixture `d`); layer 4 ("buggy code on inputs the requirement **never states**... uncatchable by any inspection... the real ceiling") is the distinct, **never-tested** case. The spike itself does not call layer 4 "false-adequate" — that extension is ADR-048's.
- **Claim (implicit):** "False-adequate" consistently names one phenomenon.
- **Evidence gap:** In Context, "false-adequate" names the case the judge *successfully caught* (spike's layer 3 / fixture `d`). In Decision-4 and Consequences, "the false-adequate ceiling" names the *structurally uncatchable* case (spike's layer 4, never tested). These are the spike's own layers 3 and 4 — a caught case and an uncaught case — collapsed under one label. A reader moving between the spike log and the ADR could reasonably conflate the demonstrated success with the standing gap, in either direction.
- **Recommendation:** Use distinct terms for the two layers (e.g., retain "false-adequate" for the caught, requirement-stated-but-missed case per the spike's own fixture naming, and introduce a separate term — "the unstated-input ceiling" or similar — for the un-spikeable layer-4 case) so the two are not conflated under one label.
- **Routing:** N/A.

**P2-5. "Generalizing to the conjunction of the gate's signals" (§Decision-1) extends a validated two-signal AND to an implied N-signal AND across the full ladder without examining how conjunctive gating scales as rungs are added.**

- **Location:** ADR-048 §Decision-1 (line 37): "`accept = tests_pass AND tests_adequate`, **generalizing to the conjunction of the gate's signals**." Compare §Decision-4: additional ladder rungs (static analysis, a code-intent judge, the oracle rung) are added "on demonstrated need."
- **Claim:** The two-signal AND structure generalizes cleanly to however many signals the ladder eventually adds.
- **Evidence gap:** A strict AND across more signals compounds false-rejection risk (each additional imperfect gate is one more chance to reject a genuinely good output) — a different failure-mode profile than the two-signal case the spike tested. The ADR does not state whether additional rungs join the same strict conjunction, some weighted/hierarchical composition, or something else; "generalizing to the conjunction" reads as though the answer is obviously "more of the same AND," which is an unexamined scaling assumption.
- **Recommendation:** Note that the composition rule for additional ladder rungs (strict AND vs. some other combination) is itself an open design question, not settled by the two-signal spike result.
- **Routing:** N/A.

## Section 2: Framing Audit

### Question 1: What alternative framings did the evidence support?

**Alternative framing A — specification-quality-primary, not verifier-architecture-primary.** The spike's own caveat ("Judge power scales with requirement specificity... a vaguer requirement might not prime the judge") suggests the load-bearing variable in the one case where the judge did something impressive (catching the century-rule coverage gap) was less "the judge is a good verifier" and more "the requirement happened to spell out the case that mattered." Under this framing, §5's acceptance-criteria-as-contract lever is not a secondary force multiplier on a primary two-signal gate (as ADR-048's ordering — Decision-1 headline, Decision-5 fifth) — it is *the* primary lever, with the executor+judge gate as necessary-but-largely-inert infrastructure absent a well-specified requirement. **Belief-mapping:** for ADR-048's chosen ordering (gate-primary, criteria-secondary) to be right, a reader needs to believe the judge's coverage-detection competence is intrinsic to the judge/architecture rather than almost entirely mediated by whether the specific requirement names the case in question — a belief the spike's own single data point (one fixture, one fully-explicit requirement, no comparison fixture with a vaguer requirement and the same bug) does not establish either way, and which the spike's hedged language leans against.

**Alternative framing B — discrimination-established, independence-deferred.** Rather than folding "independence-via-isolation" into "accepted... spike-grounded" architecture (§Conditional Acceptance), the evidence supports treating the spike's contribution as ensemble-over-frontier *discrimination* evidence (strong: a bounded 8B judge classified four static fixtures correctly with legible reasoning) while explicitly naming independence-against-a-live-builder as an unstarted BUILD/PLAY validation target — parallel to how judge-reliability-across-runs is already named as validation-pending. **Belief-mapping:** for the dominant framing to be right, a reader needs to believe architectural isolation (no builder context/reasoning passed) is sufficient to guarantee independence in practice, without any test of whether the produced-artifact channel or shared-model-weight correlation could carry builder influence — see P1-1.

**Alternative framing C — AUQ/HTC as a candidate third orthogonal signal, not an excluded one.** ADR-046's own language ("catches contract-conforming-but-anomalous generations a deterministic output check cannot see") supports treating trajectory confidence the same way ADR-048 treats the judge: an imperfect signal, anchored by determinism, included rather than excluded. **Belief-mapping:** for ADR-048's exclusion to be right, a reader needs to believe "blind to confident-wrong" is a categorically worse failure mode than "correlated-error-prone" (the judge's own named weakness) — a distinction ADR-048 asserts but does not argue. See P1-2.

### Question 2: What truths were available but not featured?

- **The spike's static-fixture, no-live-builder methodology** — present in the spike's own Setup/Method sections, not stated anywhere in ADR-048. Feeds P1-1.
- **ADR-046's explicit complementarity argument for AUQ/HTC** ("a different signal type... not assumed subsumed by the seat contract... a named candidate for Q2 grounded acceptance") — present in required source material, not engaged by ADR-048's Decision-3 demotion. Feeds P1-2.
- **ADR-014's full AUQ/HTC feature composition** (verbalized confidence + entropy + attention-weight distributions + decision-confidence trajectories + a defined entropy-collapse Abstain criterion) — present in ADR-014, reduced to "log-probability entropy" in ADR-048. Feeds P2-3.
- **ADR-046's seat-contract mechanism and its "contract assertions are a large part" framing of the accept signal** — present in required source material, unaddressed in ADR-048's Decision-1. Feeds P1-3.
- **The spike's `(probe)` label on fixture `d` and its use of a second, distinct toy task** — present in the spike's own fixture table, not distinguished from the pre-registered F1–F3 result in ADR-048. Feeds P2-1.
- **The spike's hedged ("might not") language on requirement-specificity** — present in the spike's Caveats section, hardened into an unqualified "scales with" claim in ADR-048 Decision-5. Feeds P2-2.

### Question 3: What would change if the dominant framing were inverted?

**Dominant framing:** grounded acceptance is achieved through a composed, architecturally-independent two-signal verification gate (executor + judge); acceptance criteria are a secondary force multiplier (§Decision-5, fifth of five decisions); trajectory confidence and richer signals are advisory-only or deferred.

**Inverted framing:** the acceptance decision's actual reliability is dominated by specification quality (a content variable neither the executor nor the judge supplies on its own); independence is an assumed property of an architecturally-isolated-but-never-adversarially-exercised gate rather than a demonstrated one; and an additional orthogonal signal (AUQ/HTC) that the corpus's own immediately-prior ADR named as catching a distinct failure class is excluded without being weighed against the same anchoring logic used to justify the judge's own inclusion.

Under this inversion:
- **Claims that weaken:** Decision-1's "the conjunction is the gate" headline (the conjunction's judge-half is largely inert without a specific-enough requirement); the Provenance check's "independence-via-isolation follow[s] from the Q2 grounding spike" (the spike tested discrimination on static fixtures, not independence against a live builder); Decision-3's categorical trajectory-confidence exclusion (asserted, not argued, against a counter-consideration on the record).
- **Claims that strengthen:** §5's acceptance-criteria-as-contract — under the inversion this becomes the ADR's central, load-bearing decision rather than its fifth and least-elaborated one.
- **Evidence that becomes more salient:** the spike's own hedges (single-sample, requirement-specificity-contingent, probe-not-pre-registered); ADR-046's AUQ/HTC complementarity note.
- **Evidence that becomes less salient:** the specific mechanics of seat isolation (still a real, useful design property, just no longer doing the evidentiary work "spike-grounded" implies).
- **What the ADR would need to address:** a design response to weak or underspecified acceptance criteria — the ordinary case in practice, not the spike's unusually explicit one — since the entire coverage-catching claim was demonstrated only under a requirement that happened to name the exact rule that mattered.

- Framing C / P1-2 — AUQ/HTC's exclusion is asserted against an on-the-record complementarity argument (ADR-046) that the ADR's own inclusion-logic (applied to the judge) would seem to support extending, at least in anchored form.

Recommend acknowledging in §5 (or a new framing note) that the spike's single positive coverage-detection result is better read as evidence for "requirement specificity is necessary" than as evidence for "the judge architecture is broadly competent," and that this bears on how much weight the ladder's later rungs should place on judge-side improvements versus specification-elicitation tooling.
