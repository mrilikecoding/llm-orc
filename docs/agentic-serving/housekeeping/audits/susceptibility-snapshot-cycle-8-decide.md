## Interpretation

Split the session's output into two populations before judging it as one thing.

**The bulk of the new scenario/interaction-spec content is driver-derived, not drafting-time synthesis.** I spot-checked the load-bearing framings named in the dispatch: classify→seat→marshal traces to ADR-046 §1 verbatim; `accept = tests_pass AND tests_adequate` with orthogonal executor/judge catches is a near-quote of ADR-048 §1; the thin-criteria degradation scenario matches ADR-048 §2's "weakens honestly to 'runs and is non-trivially tested'" almost word for word; the unstated-input ceiling scenario uses ADR-048 §5's own term; the AS-2 registry-admission and no-auto-promotion scenarios trace to ADR-047 §1 and §5 respectively. None of these overreach their source ADRs. This is what earned confidence looks like: the scenarios read like formalization, not invention.

**One Feature block is different in kind, and it is the one the dispatch flagged.** The "Grounded-Acceptance Gate" applicability note and its lead scenario resolve a question ADR-048 explicitly left open — I grepped ADR-046 and ADR-048 for "opt-in," "non-build," and "inert" in the context of the accept gate: neither term appears. ADR-048's own Context section says gate-*placement* is not decided there; it decides gate *composition* (what the gate checks) and *independence* (how). So this resolution is genuinely new synthesis performed at the DECIDE→ARCHITECT gate, not a restatement of a prior decision — confirming the dispatch's framing.

Is the synthesis grounded or absorbed unexamined? Partially each, and the two halves should be scored separately:

1. **The build/non-build split (gate presence follows classify's routing, not criteria richness) is sound, low-risk reuse.** Classify already discriminates build vs. non-build turns for the marshal step (ADR-046 §1); reusing that same discriminator for gate placement adds no new machinery and is architecturally clean. This half earns its confidence.

2. **The claim that presence should be uniform across *all* build turns regardless of criteria richness is weaker than the note implies.** ADR-048 §2 says thin criteria degrade the gate's judge-half toward triviality-detection, with the executor remaining the anchor. That text is genuinely ambiguous on the presence question: it supports "the gate still catches something under thin criteria, so keep it on" exactly as well as it supports "the gate barely does anything beyond what the executor alone provides under thin criteria, so the marginal value of the judge-half may not be worth its latency/round cost there." The scenario note picks the first reading and states it as though ADR-048 §2 settled it. It did not; the note's own label ("DECIDE gate resolution") is honest about this, but the scenario prose does not carry the same hedge ADR-048 itself uses for adjacent unproven claims.

3. **The "stochastic carve-out" rejection conflates two different opt-in shapes.** An LLM *judging* criteria richness to decide gate presence would indeed be exactly what determinism-over-carve-outs forbids. But an opt-in gated on a *deterministic, structural* signal — e.g., "were acceptance criteria supplied at all" — is not a stochastic carve-out and was not examined as a separate alternative before being dismissed with the rest. This is the shallow-steelman signal the dispatch asked me to assess: it is real, not a false alarm.

The agent's "your instinct reframes the fork better than I posed it" lands at exactly the moment this synthesis gets accepted into the artifact, which is a validation-adjacent phrase at a decision-adoption boundary — the classic FF1 shape from this corpus's prior loop-back snapshots (cf. `susceptibility-snapshot-cycle-7-loopback9-decide.md`, which names the same "rapid-integration-outrunning-examination" pattern across loop-backs #7-#9). What distinguishes this instance from a clean rubber stamp is that the agent surfaced two real counter-tensions (non-build inertness; round-multiplier bounding) in the same breath, and the practitioner's final acceptance was bounded ("if rounds are short... and well-scoped, I'm fine"), not unconditional. That is closer to earned convergence than pure accommodation — but it is convergence on the *round-count* tension only. The *presence-vs-richness* tension was resolved by the reframe itself, not independently re-tested afterward the way the round-count tension was.

**Amplifier from phase position.** DECIDE sits mid-gradient (more resistant than RESEARCH/DISCOVER, less resistant than BUILD), but `scenarios.md` is explicitly the driving behavioral contract for BUILD, and nothing in the artifact visually distinguishes "this scenario formalizes an ADR's converged, argument-audited Decision text" from "this scenario formalizes a gate-conversation resolution that has not been through the argument-audit cycle the ADRs themselves went through." ADR-046/047/048 each carry a Provenance check subsection precisely to make drafting-time synthesis visible as labeled content (per this cycle's own established convention, ADR-063-070's origin per the dispatch). `scenarios.md`'s applicability note has a date and a label ("DECIDE gate resolution") but no equivalent driver-vs-synthesis breakdown. The absence of that breakdown, on an artifact that otherwise reads with uniform confidence, is itself the signal the dispatch asked me to watch for.

## Recommendation

**Grounding Reframe recommended — narrow scope, in-cycle applicable.**

**What is uncertain:** whether the accept gate should run unconditionally on every build turn regardless of acceptance-criteria richness, or whether a *deterministic* criteria-presence check (not a stochastic richness judgment) could gate the gate itself on genuinely thin-criteria build turns — trading a small amount of ceiling for latency/round-count savings, without violating determinism-over-carve-outs the way a richness-scored opt-in would.

**The concrete grounding action:** ADR-048's own grounding spike (`essays/research-logs/cycle-8-spike-q2-grounded-acceptance.md`) already has fixtures spanning correct / trivially-tested / wrong-code / coverage-gap outputs under a stated-criteria condition. A cheap follow-up run — same fixtures, same judge, but with the acceptance criteria withheld or thinned to see what the judge-half actually adds beyond the executor on those specific fixtures, plus a latency/round count comparison — would tell you directly whether default-on is paying a real cost for near-zero marginal benefit on thin-criteria turns, or whether the judge-half still catches enough to justify uniform presence. This reuses existing fixtures; it is not new spike infrastructure.

**Where to record it:** the three-way distinction (driver-derived / gate-conversation-synthesized-and-unfalsified / deferred-to-BUILD) that ADR-048 applies to itself via its Provenance check is not present on `scenarios.md`'s applicability note. Adding an equivalent one- or two-line marker there — even without re-running the spike first — would at minimum make the epistemic status visible to ARCHITECT and BUILD, matching the standard the ADRs already hold themselves to.

**What the practitioner is building on without this grounding:** BUILD would wire the accept gate as unconditional on every build turn's marshal boundary. If the deterministic-presence-check alternative turns out to be cheaper and equally sound on thin-criteria turns, that option is foreclosed without having been tested, and un-wiring it later means touching BUILD-phase control-path code — ADR-048's own phrase for the class of change the determinism-over-carve-outs lens is most protective of — rather than editing a DECIDE-phase document.
