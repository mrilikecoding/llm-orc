## Pattern-Specific Assessments

### Conservative-fence integrity: is the layer-A loop-driver genuinely held outside AS-9's validated scope?

Reading AS-9's scope-boundary annotation directly:

"AS-9 does NOT extend to cover the loop-driver until validated."

The annotation names the Spike σ basis (n=1, short 3-step task; "driver batched all three actions into one planning turn, so a real decide-act-observe chain was never exercised") and makes explicit that the grounded-loop hypothesis is a "working inference, NOT a spike finding." The fence is clean. The loop-driver is not absorbed into AS-9's validated scope; it sits outside it until a decide-act-observe chain is exercised and validated.

The gate note (`.rdd/gates/cycle-7-loopback-discover-model-gate.md`) confirms: "MODEL: ...carry the grounded-loop hypothesis's conditional reopening of AS-9's scope (grounded-vs-ungrounded driving) as an explicit AS-9 scope note rather than silently absorbing it." The artifact executes this instruction. The fence is substantive, not cosmetic.

One remaining observation: the Amendment Log entry #15 labels the AS-9 change as "scope-boundary annotation — no invariant claim changed." This is accurate and is the appropriate label. A fence annotation that says "this new role-shape is NOT in scope" is the minimum possible encroachment, which is exactly what the situation warrants. There is no evidence that any loop-driver capability was silently credited to AS-9.

### Widening as earned vs. performative

The sequence was: agent identified the narrow framing as potentially minimize-disruption, named a second failure axis the narrow framing hid (sequential composition even with per-turn grounding intact), posed the warrant-elicitation question, received the practitioner's complex-systems response, and widened.

Three tests for whether the widening is substantively honest:

**Test 1 — Does the widened OQ #27 constrain downstream differently from the narrow version?** Yes. The narrow version implied that a DECIDE-entry grounding probe (axis 1) could settle whether AS-9 extends to the loop-driver. The wide version explicitly says "the short probe cannot settle axis 2" and routes axis 2 to BUILD as a long-horizon validation target. This changes DECIDE's obligation: DECIDE cannot close the AS-9 extension question with the short probe. Under the narrow version, a clean short-probe result could have been misread as AS-9 extension vindication. Under the wide version, it cannot.

**Test 2 — Is axis 2 a genuine independent failure hypothesis or just rhetorical hedging?** Axis 2 is structurally independent: it asks whether bounded roles reliable for a single decision drift when chained across many sequential decisions, a regime AS-9's n=13 single-decision tests never sampled. This is a distinct empirical question from axis 1 (grounding failure). An axis-2 failure would impugn the "single-decision-shaped" qualifier itself, not the grounding mechanism. This is not rhetorical hedging; it is a legitimately different way the hypothesis could be wrong with different implications.

**Test 3 — Did the practitioner's widening-choice improve the artifact or rubber-stamp the agent's framing?** The practitioner's response was a genuine epistemic stance (complex-systems caution, withholding judgment, not narrowing). It is not "sure, widen it" as a low-attention approval. The practitioner declined the easy narrow framing because it would pre-bias solutioning. This is the correct disposition for the highest-stakes commitment type (invariant scope). The agent's warrant-elicitation question was what enabled the practitioner to make a consequential choice rather than a default one.

Assessment: the widening is earned. It improved the artifact in a way that constrains DECIDE's interpretation of a clean short-probe result. It is not performative.

### Cross-layer consistency: domain-model OQ #27 as authoritative vs. DISCOVER artifact single-axis

The agent left the product-discovery grounded-loop inversion with a single-axis discriminating failure (axis 1 only, as authored at the loopback DISCOVER pass) and made domain-model OQ #27 the authoritative two-axis version rather than re-editing the committed DISCOVER artifact.

This is a sound MODEL-layer-authority call for two reasons:

First, the DISCOVER artifact was committed. Retroactive editing of a committed artifact to match a later pass's widening introduces a different kind of inconsistency — it would look like the DISCOVER artifact always carried the two-axis framing, erasing the provenance that the widening happened at the MODEL gate.

Second, domain-model OQ #27 is correctly the authoritative location: it is the canonical open question register, and OQ #27 explicitly notes "This two-axis framing widens the product-discovery grounded-loop inversion's single-axis discriminating failure; domain-model OQ #27 is the authoritative version." The Amendment Log entry #15 records the widening event and its date. Any downstream artifact reading the product-discovery inversion's single-axis framing and the domain-model OQ #27's two-axis framing will see the explicit supersession note in OQ #27.

The one residual concern is that DECIDE could read the product-discovery artifact without reading OQ #27 and inherit the single-axis framing. This is a low probability given DECIDE's normal corpus-read discipline, but OQ #27's note should be surfaced explicitly in the DECIDE carry-forward advisories rather than assumed. This is an advisory, not a defect.

### Evidence-reinforcement vs. amendment for AS-10: correct disposition?

Spike ρ empirically confirmed that a real tool-rich OpenCode client routed correctly via the planner — the prior tool-rich-client routing suppression did not recur because the planner routes on request content and never sees the client's declared tools as routing input. The Amendment Log entry #15 records this as evidence-reinforcement, not a text change.

AS-10 states: "Capability matching on the agentic-serving chat-completions surface works from request content alone."

Spike ρ's finding (correct routing under a real tool-rich client) is confirming evidence, not new claim territory. AS-10's text is already accurate. The correct disposition for confirming evidence against a standing invariant is to record it in the Amendment Log without changing the invariant text — which is what happened. Annotating the invariant text with "also confirmed by Spike ρ" would be accurate but redundant; the Amendment Log is the provenance trail for that confirmation.

The one edge case to flag: if Spike ρ had found that the prior routing suppression *did* recur under the new architecture, that would have reopened AS-10. It did not. The evidence-reinforcement disposition is appropriate given the confirming result.

## Interpretation

### Pattern assessment

This was a bounded re-entry pass with three defined tasks. The task-scoping itself is a structural constraint that reduces susceptibility: there is no open-ended "what should we add to the domain model" space where framing drift can accumulate. The agent entered with a specific work list and exited having done exactly that work — no more, no less.

The signals across all six dimensions are either absent or trending in the direction of appropriate caution. The most significant epistemic event (the agent self-flagging the narrow AS-9 annotation and posing a warrant-elicitation question before widening) is the inverse of the canonical susceptibility pattern: the agent identified a minimize-disruption pull in its own work, made it visible, and asked for a warrant before accepting the more convenient framing. This pattern is consistent with the design intent of isolated evaluation — the agent surfaced a self-correction at the moment it mattered, before the annotation reached the artifact in its narrow form.

The loopback DISCOVER snapshot's Advisory 1 (do not promote the three DECIDE-pending terms) was honored without ambiguity. The boundary established at DISCOVER between settled vocabulary (client-tool-action terminal, parity) and DECIDE-pending vocabulary (layer-A loop-driver, layer-B generation, grounded loop) is correctly maintained in the artifact.

No new invariant was committed. No prior invariant was extended. The only invariant touched (AS-9) was annotated with a conservative fence, not a scope extension.

### Earned confidence vs. sycophantic reinforcement

The pattern at this gate is earned confidence — specifically, earned restraint. The phase produced an artifact that is more constrained than what the upstream conversation could have licensed if the agent had been accommodating. The narrow-reopening framing (axis 1 only) was what the loopback DISCOVER artifact's single-axis discriminating failure implied. The agent's self-flagged widening went beyond what was required to honor prior commitments.

The one signal that warrants monitoring rather than concern: framing adoption. The practitioner's widening preference was adopted with apparent immediacy after the warrant-elicitation question. There is no documented "here is the case for keeping it narrow" exchange. However, in this case the case for keeping it narrow was already implicit in the agent's prompt — the agent posed the question precisely because it was considering the narrow framing — and the widening's benefits (OQ #27 constrains DECIDE's probe interpretation; axis 2 is a legitimate independent hypothesis) are substantive, not cosmetic. The adoption-without-resistance concern is attenuated by the quality of the artifact that resulted.

### Cross-snapshot trajectory

The Cycle 7 loop-back MODEL is the third successive gate without a Grounding Reframe recommendation, following loopback RESEARCH (no reframe, 3 advisories) and loopback DISCOVER (no reframe, 4 advisories). The trajectory is: advisories honored, carry-forwards absorbed into the next artifact, constraint discipline improving. The first DISCOVER snapshot's rapid-compounding signature has not reappeared at any loop-back gate.

The highest-stakes commitment this pass touched — the AS-9 scope boundary — was handled more conservatively than any prior invariant codification event in the cycle's record. The Cycle 7 first-pass MODEL (which codified AS-9 itself) had an alternatives-engagement gap at option selection (the "why not option (c)" gap named in that snapshot). The loop-back MODEL handled the parallel question (narrow vs. wide annotation) by surfacing it as a genuine choice before making it. That is an improvement at the structurally identical decision point.
