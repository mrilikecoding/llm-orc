## Section 2: Framing Audit

### Question 1: What alternative framings did the evidence support?

**The revision fixed R1's Framing B (the orchestrator-actor referent question) at the row-classification level but not at the content level.** R1's P2-6 named six rows presupposing an orchestrator actor the architecture may have dissolved, and recommended either naming a declarative home for each or moving them to `[read]`. The revision moved four rows to flat "superseded" (003, 006, 008, 011) and hedged two (004, 005 → "mechanism superseded; concern re-homes," explicit `[read]` follow-on). That structural move was the right instinct — but "is this ADR's actor-dependent mechanism superseded" and "does everything this ADR decided depend on the actor" are different questions, and the revision answered the first while treating it as if it had answered the second. Three of the four flat-superseded rows (003, 006, 011) turn out to bundle actor-specific content with actor-agnostic content that doesn't survive the bundling (P1-1, P1-2, P2-1 above). The alternative framing the evidence supports: for any row being moved out of `[confirm]`, ask "does *every* decided component of this ADR belong to the dissolved actor, or only some" — component-level, not ADR-level — rather than treating supersession as all-or-nothing once actor-dependence is established for *any* part of the document.
