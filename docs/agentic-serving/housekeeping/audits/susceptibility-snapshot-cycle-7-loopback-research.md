## Interpretation

The signals collectively describe a phase where the two most consequential susceptibility moments (embedded conclusion in the research question; inference-as-framing at the gate) were both caught and corrected — one by the methods reviewer before spikes ran, one by the practitioner at the reflection gate. Both corrections materially altered the artifact: the first produced Spike π Phase A as a genuine test of the alternative rather than a confirmation run; the second produced W8.6 with the candidates-not-findings language and the grounded-loop hypothesis as an explicitly flagged working inference rather than a spike-grounded conclusion.

The framing-adoption signal warrants specific attention. The "incomplete, not wrong; aligned with ADR-027's philosophy" framing entered via the cycle-status BUILD-surfaced finding, which the agent wrote or curated, and was not independently tested during the loop-back re-entry. Argument audit R1's Alternative A ("ADR-027 is wrong, not just incomplete") and R4's inverted framing ("the terminal gap reveals ADR-027 solved the wrong problem") each named this as a viable alternative. Neither was investigated as a live hypothesis; both were recorded as framing notes. The consequence for DISCOVER → DECIDE → ARCHITECT: ADR-027 amendment vs. ADR-027 replacement is a design question the artifact leaves to DECIDE (W8.6 says "DECIDE/ARCHITECT select"), but the artifact's framing tilts toward amendment. A DECIDE phase that inherits only the artifact without the audit alternatives would not readily surface the replacement option.

This is a moderate susceptibility signal, not a strong one. The agent did not advocate for the "incomplete, not wrong" framing as a conclusion; it is present as background assumption. The corrective evidence is that the artifact preserves the inverted-framing alternatives in audit documents that are part of the corpus and should flow to DISCOVER. The risk is that DISCOVER-as-update-mode, processing the artifact without re-reading the audit alternatives, treats the amendment framing as settled.

The solution-space narrowing signal for the layer-A seat is clean. Three candidates with parallel AS-9 notes, no selection, and explicit BUILD-phase discriminating evidence required. The practitioner's late-phase question ("is there an observation worth one more spike before we switch to the amendment?") surfaced by the agent as a genuine Patch-Switching decision point, and acted on (Spike σ added), demonstrates the practitioner was engaged as a co-investigator, not a passive acceptor.

**Earned confidence vs. sycophantic reinforcement assessment:** The substantive practitioner engagement — constraint-removal correcting the geography premise, sharp pushback at the gate correcting the grounded-loop-as-established-framing, active decision at σ-loop close — is the kind of challenge that distinguishes earned from reinforced confidence. The gate correction in particular (practitioner catching an unflagged agent inference) is the pattern most resistant to sycophantic drift: the practitioner independently questioned what the agent foregrounded rather than assenting to a presented framing. C8 in its final form is tighter than C8-before-gate-correction in a way that reflects a real epistemic event, not a cosmetic revision. The pattern is consistent with earned confidence at the overall level.

The one residual concern is not a sycophancy finding but a carry-forward advisory: the "incomplete, not wrong" framing that entered via the cycle-status artifact was not subjected to independent examination. It is plausible and well-supported; it is also the framing that minimizes the scope of upstream design change required. The coincidence of "the adopted framing" and "the framing that requires the least rework" is worth naming for DISCOVER.

## Recommendation

**No Grounding Reframe warranted for blocking DISCOVER entry.** The loop-back's two embedded-conclusion moments were caught and corrected before the artifact closed. The practitioner engagement is substantive and the corrective direction is genuine. The artifact's scope flags and candidates-not-findings language are load-bearing enough that downstream phases cannot read W8.6 as settled results.

**Advisory for DISCOVER (update mode) — carry-forward:**

1. **The "incomplete, not wrong" framing is adopted, not derived.** The cycle-status building block ("ADR-027 is incomplete, not wrong; the fix aligns with its philosophy") entered the artifact without independent examination against the alternative (ADR-027 may be wrong for the north-star use case). Argument audit R1 (Alt-A, inverted framing) and R4 (Question 3) named the alternative. DISCOVER's update to the Skill Orchestration User and Tool User mental models is the natural place to ask whether "amendment" or "redesign" is the right frame for what ADR-027 lacks. The question is actionable: what does the Skill Orchestration User's mental model require from the endpoint's behavior across a 20-turn RDD session? If the answer is closer to "conversational loop participant" than "pipeline with a delivery stage," the amendment framing loses ground. DISCOVER should surface this explicitly rather than inheriting the framing by default.

2. **The grounded-loop hypothesis needs a discriminating question, not just a BUILD-phase target.** W8.6 correctly defers the seat-filler decision to DECIDE/ARCHITECT, but the hypothesis (grounded per-turn role is structurally different from the ungrounded multi-dispatch role AS-9 removed) will shape how ADR-027 is amended. DISCOVER's stakeholder inhabitation is a natural place to ask: if the layer-A seat is filled by a model (candidate 1), what failure mode would a practitioner observe that would signal AS-9 is being reopened? Naming the observable failure before BUILD runs is cheaper than diagnosing it after 20+ turns of a long-horizon task.

3. **The F-ρ.1 artifact bridge gap should surface in DISCOVER's interaction model, not only in ARCHITECT's module decomposition.** F-ρ.1 (the artifact store → tool_call content bridge) is currently framed as a structural gap for ARCHITECT to address. But the Skill Orchestration User and Tool User mental models implicitly assume the deliverable arrives at their machine in a useful form. DISCOVER's update should verify that assumption explicitly and name the interaction shape: ensemble produces → server reads artifact → terminal marshals → client executes → client observes. If that shape does not match what the user mental model expects, the interaction spec needs to account for it before ARCHITECT decomposes the implementation.
