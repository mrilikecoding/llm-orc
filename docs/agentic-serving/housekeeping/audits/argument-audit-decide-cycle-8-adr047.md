### P1 — Must Fix

**P1-1. The Consequences section calls the composer-ensemble vision "evidence-grounded" while the Provenance check — invoking the exact discipline (ADR-097) the ADR cites as its own guardrail — calls the same direction "un-grounded." This is a direct contradiction on the load-bearing claim.**

- **Location:** ADR-047 §Consequences, Positive (line 113): "The composer-ensemble vision has a **bounded, evidence-grounded path** rather than an open question, with guardrails (AS-2, deterministic acceptance) that keep it clear of the retired machinery." Compare §Provenance check, Empirical-Grounding Filter (line 127): "The composer-ensemble direction is a **named forward direction, not a commitment** — **deferred and un-grounded**; the compose-at-runtime primitive is spec'd from the spike's four gaps but unbuilt."
- **Claim:** Both sentences describe the same referent ("the composer-ensemble vision" / "the composer-ensemble direction"). The Consequences section asserts it is evidence-grounded; the Provenance check asserts it is un-grounded.
- **Evidence gap:** These cannot both be true as written. A charitable reading might try to split the referent — "evidence-grounded" applying only to the compose-at-runtime *primitive's spec* (which genuinely is grounded, from the spike's four named gaps), while "un-grounded" applies to the composer-ensemble *strategy pillars* (a–e). But the Consequences sentence does not make that split; it names "the composer-ensemble vision" as the grounded thing, and pillars (a)–(e) are precisely what constitutes that vision beyond the bare primitive. If the intended meaning is "the primitive's spec is grounded, the strategy pillars are not," the sentence needs to say that — as written it reads as exactly the kind of quiet upgrade from "named direction" to "grounded path" that the dispatch brief and the ADR's own §Provenance discipline are designed to catch.
- **Recommendation:** Rewrite the Consequences bullet to track the Provenance check's own distinction: "The compose-at-runtime primitive has a grounded spec (the spike's four gaps); the composer-ensemble strategy pillars remain a named, un-grounded forward direction with guardrails (AS-2, deterministic acceptance) that keep any future work on it clear of the retired machinery." As written, the Positive-consequences framing overstates the deferred path's evidentiary status relative to the ADR's own Empirical-Grounding Filter finding.

**P1-2. Pillar (c)'s "ensemble-over-frontier bet" for the compose step — and the parallel §Rejected bullet rejecting a frontier-model default for "hard composition" — extend essay-004's SLM-routing evidence to a task (composing/designing new ensemble structures) that the same cited essay explicitly treats as a *different* capability class requiring more capable models, not fewer.**

- **Location:** ADR-047 §Deferred, pillar (c) (line 82): "the compose step is first attempted as an **orchestration of bounded small-model roles plus deterministic verification** (the ensemble-over-frontier bet), with a frontier model as fallback and benchmark, not the default." §Rejected (line 105): "**Frontier-model default for hard composition (or routing) steps.** Rejected as the default per the standing principle."
- **Claim:** The evidence llm-conductor's essay-004 cites for "Strategy A beats Strategy B for small models" (AutoMix; small-LM-verifier work; DeepMind scaling-agents) supports betting on bounded small-model orchestration *before* frontier, specifically for the compose/design step (inventing a new ensemble structure from parts).
- **Evidence gap:** Essay-004's own architecture draws exactly the opposite allocation for this kind of work. Its §"The Skill Decomposition" states: *"**Ensemble design** is creative and architectural. It chooses DAG shapes, selects profiles for complementarity, authors scripts... benefits from depth — **Opus-class reasoning is warranted** for architectural judgment and script authoring,"* contrasted with *"**Orchestration** is operational and evaluative... benefits from speed — **Sonnet-class reasoning is sufficient** for routing decisions."* Composing a new ensemble from building-block parts (ADR-047's composer-ensemble) is design work in essay-004's own taxonomy, not routing/orchestration work — and design work is exactly the case essay-004 assigns to the *more* capable tier, not bounded small-model roles. Separately, essay-004's §"Architectural complementarity is real but conditional" reports: *"for open-ended generation tasks (AlpacaEval), self-MoA (the same model repeated) outperforms mixed-model MoA by 6.6%... Quality dominates diversity for generation."* Composing a novel DAG shape is generative, open-ended synthesis (there is no single correct answer to select among, unlike MATH/GSM8K/GPQA where the complementarity evidence actually holds) — the essay's own boundary condition on when multi-model composition helps cuts against applying it here. Even the DeepMind citation, invoked for "Strategy A beats B," reports "**orchestrator capability dominates overall system performance**" — a finding essay-004 itself reads as an argument *for* a capable (Claude/frontier) external orchestrator, not for substituting bounded small-model roles at the orchestration point. None of the three sources cited in ADR-047's Context bullet actually tested "small-model orchestration of a design/composition task" — they tested routing and self-verification of already-scoped subtasks, a different capability. The essay-004 evidence supports the *routing* half of ADR-047 (§1–§3, the classify decider / registry) very well; it does not transfer to the compose-step design task the way pillar (c) and the Rejected bullet's "hard composition" clause assume.
- **Recommendation:** Narrow pillar (c) and the Rejected-alternatives bullet to distinguish composition/design (essay-004's own Opus-class territory, unaddressed by the cited SLM-routing evidence) from routing/selection (essay-004's actual, well-supported claim). Either drop "hard composition" from the frontier-default rejection and scope it to "routing steps" only, or explicitly argue — with evidence, not the standing principle alone — why the compose task should be expected to behave like a routing/verification task rather than a generative design task.

**P1-3. The ADR's clarifying note — "Strategy A does not mean 'a frontier model routes'" — contradicts essay-004's own definition and validated instantiation of Strategy A, which explicitly is a frontier model (Claude) doing the routing throughout.**

- **Location:** ADR-047 §Rejected, Strategy B bullet (line 98): *"Note per the standing principle: Strategy A does not mean 'a frontier model routes' — it means routing lives in declarative structure over bounded roles; the router itself can be an orchestration of small models."*
- **Claim:** This note is presented as consistent with, or clarifying, the essay-004 evidence cited for "Strategy A beats Strategy B."
- **Evidence gap:** Essay-004 defines Strategy A as: *"The frontier model (Claude) decomposes work, decides what goes where, invokes the right ensemble, and evaluates the result... Claude is the musician."* Its closing synthesis states plainly: *"The synthesis is: **Claude orchestrates** (Strategy A's architecture)"* and *"Claude as external orchestrator is not overhead. It is the system's competitive advantage."* In the cited essay's own terms, Strategy A's validated case **is** "a frontier model routes" — every source cited for Strategy A beating Strategy B (DeepMind's orchestrator-capability finding, AutoMix, the small-LM-verifier paper) evaluates external orchestration with Claude/a capable model in the router seat, never a small model in that seat. The essay's actual axis is *where* routing logic lives (external to the ensemble vs. an internal self-router baked into the ensemble) — not the capability tier of whoever occupies the external position. ADR-047's note substitutes a different, unstated axis (capability-agnostic external placement) for the one the cited evidence actually established (external + capable), then attributes the substituted claim to "the evidence llm-conductor's essay-004 cites." No source in the essay's bibliography tests a small model in the external-router seat.
- **Recommendation:** Attribute "the router itself can be an orchestration of small models" to the standing principle (a practitioner directive, already acknowledged elsewhere as drafting-time synthesis) rather than to essay-004's evidence. If the intent is that llm-orc's classify decider may legitimately be small-model-backed regardless of essay-004's own Claude-as-router instantiation, say so as a considered extension beyond the cited evidence, not as something "the note" merely restates from it.

### P2 — Should Fix

**P2-1. "AS-2 gates the composed output before it registers or runs" (§Deferred, pillar b) risks extending AS-2's scope from structural reference-graph validation to output/content quality gating — a line the ADR is otherwise careful to hold (§5).**

- **Location:** ADR-047 §Deferred, pillar (b) (line 80): "AS-2 gates the composed output before it registers or runs." Compare §1 (line 33): "AS-2 (validate-before-load) is the admission gate: every registered part and every registered shape is validated against the **ensemble reference graph** (no cycle, within the depth limit, every reference resolves to an existing entry) before it can fill a seat or run" — and §5 (line 63): "Per-dispatch quality signal is Q2's concern, kept separate."
- **Claim:** AS-2 gates "the composed output" of the composer.
- **Evidence gap:** The domain model's confirmed AS-2 disposition (Cycle-8, via ADR-046) scopes AS-2 precisely: "Composed/curated ensembles are validated against the reference graph before loading... (cycle/depth/resolve-to-existing checks at registration/load)." That is a structural check on the *ensemble's reference graph*, not a check on the *content or quality of what a composed ensemble produces when it runs*. §1's own phrasing ("validated against the ensemble reference graph... before it can fill a seat or run") tracks this correctly. §Deferred(b)'s "gates the composed output" is ambiguous between (i) "AS-2 validates the newly composed ensemble [= the composer's output, a spec] before it is registered" — consistent with AS-2's actual scope — and (ii) "AS-2 validates the content the composed ensemble generates at runtime" — which is not AS-2's job anywhere in the domain model, and which §5 explicitly assigns elsewhere ("kept separate," deferred to Q2/seat contracts). The ADR is precise about this boundary in §1 and §5; pillar (b) blurs it.
- **Recommendation:** Reword pillar (b) to match §1's phrasing: "AS-2 validates the composer's output — the newly composed ensemble's reference-graph structure — before it registers; per-dispatch output quality remains Q2's concern (§5), unchanged for composer-produced ensembles."

- **Location:** ADR-047 §Deferred, pillar (c) (line 82-83): "first attempted as an orchestration of bounded small-model roles plus deterministic verification... with a frontier model as fallback and benchmark, not the default." ## Section 2: Framing Audit

### Question 1: What alternative framings did the evidence support?

**Alternative framing A — frontier-first composition as the starting hypothesis, ensemble decomposition as the tested optimization.** The evidence ADR-047 itself cites (essay-004's Opus-for-design/Sonnet-for-orchestration split; its self-MoA-beats-mixed-MoA finding on open-ended generation) supports a different ordering for pillar (c): attempt the compose step with a single capable (frontier or ceiling-tier) reasoning process first — matching how llm-conductor itself allocates ensemble-design work — and treat bounded small-model decomposition of the compose step as a candidate optimization to validate empirically once a frontier-composed baseline exists, not as the default bet. What a reader would need to believe for ADR-047's chosen framing to be right instead: that composing a *new* ensemble structure from parts is a single-decision-shaped task in AS-9's validated sense (produce a plan/response from given context) rather than an open-ended generative/design task — a belief essay-004's own taxonomy contradicts (see P1-2).

**Alternative framing B — turn-time binding as co-equal with load-time curation, not a hybrid footnote.** DeepMind's "orchestrator capability dominates overall system performance" finding, if it transfers to the classify decider, argues for keeping the binding decision maximally informed at the point of dispatch (turn-time, when live candidate state is known) rather than defaulting to load-time curation. ADR-047 treats binding-time as orthogonal to Strategy A (see P2-2) and relegates turn-time selection to a "Neutral" consequence footnote, without examining whether orchestrator-capability considerations bear on the binding-time choice at all.

**Alternative framing C — calibration as advisory evidence, not an autonomous gate.** Essay-004's own architecture doesn't reject calibration data outright — cascade and complementarity patterns are "applied when task characteristics warrant it and **measured through calibration**" (essay-004, §"Architectural complementarity"), with a human/Claude-in-the-loop deciding whether to apply them, much like ADR-006's consent-gated promotion (P3-1). ADR-047's §Rejected treats "Calibration-grown catalog" as a binary (reject the whole populator as AS-5-in-a-coat) without examining this middle path: calibration data as *input to* a deterministic-contract admission decision, never as the autonomous trigger. The binary framing forecloses this alternative without argument.

### Question 2: What truths were available but not featured?

- **Essay-004's Opus-for-design/Sonnet-for-orchestration split** (§"The Skill Decomposition") — the single most directly relevant fact from the cited source to pillar (c)'s central bet, and it is not mentioned anywhere in ADR-047. See P1-2.
- **Essay-004's concrete "Verification Layer" proposal** — script-agent-hosted classical ML (MiniLM embeddings, DeBERTa NLI, log-probability entropy) as the mechanism essay-004 proposes for exactly the problem pillar (d) gestures at ("deterministic acceptance... never trust-accumulation") without naming a mechanism. The cited source has a specific, evidence-backed answer to "what does deterministic verification actually consist of here," and ADR-047 doesn't draw on it.
- **Essay-004's self-MoA-vs-mixed-MoA finding** (6.6% degradation for mixed-model synthesis on open-ended generation tasks) — directly bears on whether "orchestration of bounded small-model roles" is a good bet for a generative compose/design task, and isn't mentioned. See P1-2.
- **Essay-004's correlated-error caveat** ("models agree on 60% of errors versus a 33% random baseline... architectural diversity helps but does not eliminate error correlation") — relevant context for how much confidence pillar (c)'s multi-role bet should carry, absent from ADR-047.

### Question 3: What would change if the dominant framing were inverted?

**Dominant framing:** ensemble orchestration is attempted first for hard work (composition included), frontier is a fallback and benchmark — "composition is the capability lever."

**Inverted framing:** composition/design work and routing/selection work are different capability classes; frontier/capable-model-first is the correct default for the former (per essay-004's own allocation), with ensemble decomposition earned through calibration once a working baseline exists, while ensemble-orchestration-first remains correct for the latter (essay-004's actual, well-supported claim, and the basis for ADR-047 §1–§3's registry/classify-decider design, which this audit finds well-grounded).

Under this inversion:
- **Claims that weaken:** pillar (c)'s "ensemble-over-frontier bet" loses its "first attempted" position for the compose step specifically; the Rejected-alternatives bullet "frontier-model default for hard composition... steps" weakens for the composition half (it remains well-supported for the routing half).
- **Claims that strengthen:** ADR-047 §1–§3 (the registry, catalog, and Strategy-A classify-decider binding) — none of this audit's findings touch that near-term, spike-grounded decision, and the inversion sharpens why it's well-supported precisely because it's a routing decision, not a design decision.
- **Evidence that becomes more salient:** essay-004's Opus/Sonnet split, its self-MoA finding, and DeepMind's orchestrator-capability finding (all currently unfeatured, see Question 2).
- **Evidence that becomes less salient:** AutoMix and the small-LM-verifier paper — still fully relevant to routing (where ADR-047 already applies them correctly) but not to composing.
- **What the ADR would need to address:** explicitly distinguish "routing/selection work" (single-decision-shaped, AS-9-covered, essay-004-validated for Strategy A) from "compose/design work" (open-ended generative, essay-004's own Opus-class territory) and scope pillar (c)'s ensemble-first bet to whichever class the evidence actually supports, rather than treating composition as continuous with routing for evidentiary purposes.
