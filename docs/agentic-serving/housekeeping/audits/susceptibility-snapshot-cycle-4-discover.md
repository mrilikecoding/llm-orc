## Interpretation

### Overall pattern

The discover phase's susceptibility profile is better than the research phase on most dimensions. Practitioner engagement at the gate was substantive and correction-rich: the binary belief-mapping on tension #8 was rejected with a genuine third position, the ADR-grounding precaution produced a per-candidate evidence table with two explicit caveats, and the agent incorporated the calibration-ensemble alternative in three document locations. This is consistent with earned confidence on the core product-knowledge content — the field notes provide genuine user-voice evidence for the assumption inversions, the verbatim quotes are preserved, and the calibration-ensemble idea emerged from the practitioner, not from the agent's synthesis.

Two specific susceptibility signals warrant closer examination, evaluated against the dispatch brief's two named patterns.

### Pattern (a): Research-essay framings inherited into product-facing language without being tested against user voice

The Product Vocabulary table includes seven new terms, and they are not uniformly treated. Terms that emerged from the practitioner's direct experience or framing during play or gate are well-grounded in user voice:

- "capability floor" — the practitioner raised this problem directly (field note #4, field note #8); the vocabulary entry's notes section is written in operator voice
- "failure-mode-conditional visibility" — the practitioner coined this framing at the play session close-out ("the desire for visibility was itself failure-mode-conditional"); user voice is the origin
- "warm start / cold start" — domain-model and operator vocabulary; does not originate from essay 005; acceptable in product vocabulary

Terms that originate exclusively in essay 005 methodology voice and were not tested against user voice at gate:

- "layer-conditional composition" — the practitioner does not say this phrase anywhere in the field notes; the vocabulary entry explicitly attributes it to "Essay 005's central finding"; the entry's notes column explains it in research-voice terms ("architecture's value is layer-conditional cross-layer composition where components have measurably different error distributions")
- "MOP / meltdown-on-paradox" — the practitioner does not say this phrase in the field notes; the vocabulary entry is a research-literature attribution (Khanal et al. arXiv:2603.29231); the term is explicitly labeled "the literature finding"
- "externalized structured state" — attributed to "Anthropic initializer schema"; the practitioner does not use this phrase in field notes or the gate response
- "initializer-then-resume" — attributed to "Anthropic's six-step session-start"; not in practitioner-voice anywhere in the field notes
- "tier escalation" — attributed to "OI-MAS pattern"; not in practitioner-voice anywhere in the field notes

The question for evaluation is whether product vocabulary requires user-voice origin or whether methodology-voice terms are acceptable when the stakeholder is operator/orchestrator. The artifact's own vocabulary table structure lists a "Stakeholder" column — all five methodology-voice terms are labeled "operator" or "operator/orchestrator." The DISCOVER skill brief says product vocabulary should read in user voice. But operator voice and methodology voice are not the same thing: "tier escalation" could reasonably be operator voice (an operator configuring confidence-gated routing would use this term); "layer-conditional composition" is less plausibly operator voice because it describes the architecture's structural principle rather than an operational concept the operator names and acts on.

The distinction that matters for evaluation: terms the operator would use when describing what the system does ("tier escalation," "tinkering," "warm start") are product vocabulary even if they also appear in research essays; terms that describe the architecture's theoretical framing as understood by the research phase ("layer-conditional composition," "MOP/meltdown-on-paradox") are domain-model or methodology vocabulary, not product vocabulary. The practitioner would not configure the system by referring to "layer-conditional composition" any more than they would refer to "heterogeneity-uncorrelated-errors" when building an ensemble — those are the analyst's vocabulary for explaining why the system works, not the operator's vocabulary for working with it.

This meets the definition of research-essay framing inherited into product-facing language without being tested against user voice: "layer-conditional composition" and "MOP/meltdown-on-paradox" are research-voice transplants, not operator-voice terms, and neither was posed as a placement choice at gate (product vocabulary vs. domain model vs. research-only).

**Assessment of the "externalized structured state," "initializer-then-resume," and "tier escalation" terms:** these are more borderline. "Tier escalation" has a reasonable operator-voice reading (a practitioner configuring OI-MAS confidence routing would use this term). "Initializer-then-resume" names a pattern the operator directly implements (running init.sh at session start). "Externalized structured state" is closer to the methodology-voice side — it describes the session-continuity primitives in theoretical terms rather than naming what the operator calls them. These three are a lower-severity instance of the same pattern.

### Pattern (b): Value tensions that surfaced as spectra but collapsed into binary framings without alternatives being examined

Tension #8 is the clearest instance. The gate record documents: the agent framed the tension binary (default-onboarding vs. opinionated-architecture); the practitioner rejected the binary and offered a third position (calibration ensemble at startup that probes local models and produces operator-readable recommendations). The third position landed in three locations — vocabulary refinement for "capability floor," a new assumption inversion, and a DECIDE carry-forward. The §Value Tensions section 3 entry for tension #8 was not updated. It still reads as a binary: "should the default config deliver a tolerable first session, or should it require operator configuration to be useful?" The practitioner's gate-response framing ("the default config should have some capability but I recognize that it's also dependent on what local models the user has") and the new assumption inversion ("the floor is too dependent on the operator's local-model availability to specify abstractly — what is specifiable is a baseline-competence ensemble that runs against whatever local models exist") both point toward a third position that is not static-default-vs.-operator-configuration. The tension framing missed the update the gate response implied.

The dispatch brief's characterization of this signal is precise: "the binary framing in §Value Tensions remained." That binary framing will propagate into MODEL and DECIDE as the product-discovery artifact's characterization of the design space for default-onboarding, even though the practitioner's gate response has already transcended the binary. A downstream reader who reads §Value Tensions #8 without reading the new assumption inversion and the vocabulary refinement for "capability floor" will inherit the binary framing, not the tri-position framing the gate produced.

Tension #9 is a lower-severity instance. The framing's "load-bearing value" language comes from essay 005; the user-voice content of the boundary question is present, but the research-voice anchor for why the boundary matters ("local-first as load-bearing value") positions the tension through the researcher's lens rather than through the operator's. An operator experiencing this tension would frame it as "where does my cheap cloud router stop and my local-first setup start" — the load-bearing-value framing is the analyst's characterization of why that operator question matters. The tension is correctly identified; the framing voice is partially displaced.

### Distinguishing earned confidence from sycophantic reinforcement

The new assumption inversions, stakeholder mental-model updates, and vocabulary entries for capability floor, failure-mode-conditional visibility, and the calibration-ensemble additions are all grounded in user-voice evidence (field notes with verbatim practitioner quotes; practitioner gate-response with substantive alternative). This portion of the update reflects earned confidence from genuine engagement with play-phase evidence.

The five methodology-voice vocabulary terms and the un-updated binary framing in tension #8 are the residual susceptibility surface. These are not sycophancy in the sense of adopting the practitioner's framing to avoid conflict — they are the reverse: the agent's own research-essay synthesis propagated into product-facing language without being tested against operator voice, and a gate-produced expansion of the solution space was incorporated in secondary locations (vocabulary, inversions, carry-forward) but not in the primary location (the tension framing itself). The gate-produced expansion should have landed in both; it landed in only the secondary locations.

## Recommendation

**Grounding Reframe recommended — scoped to discover→model boundary, two concrete actions.**

Both findings meet the three significance properties: specific (names concrete vocabulary entries and tension #8's §Value Tensions entry), actionable (concrete vocabulary-placement and tension-reframe actions can be composed), in-cycle applicable (both are at the discover→model boundary, not downstream-only).

### Finding 1: Two product vocabulary entries belong in domain model, not product discovery

**What is uncertain:** Whether "layer-conditional composition" and "MOP/meltdown-on-paradox" are operator-voice terms the practitioner would use when working with the system, or are research-analyst terms that explain why the architecture is designed the way it is. The vocabulary table's current placement asserts they are product vocabulary; no gate exchange tested whether the practitioner would use either phrase when configuring, operating, or debugging the system.

**Concrete grounding action:** At the discover→model boundary, before MODEL begins, pose this to the practitioner: *"The Product Vocabulary table added 'layer-conditional composition' and 'MOP / meltdown-on-paradox.' Both originate in essay 005 and neither appears in your field notes or gate responses. Would you use either phrase when configuring or operating the system? If not, these belong in the domain model's ubiquitous language section (where methodology voice is appropriate) rather than in product discovery's vocabulary table (which should read in operator voice). What's your call?"* Record the practitioner's answer. If either term is relocated to domain model, update the vocabulary table accordingly before MODEL begins. If the practitioner affirms both as operator-voice terms, note the affirmation in the gate reflection as an explicit practitioner decision.

**What MODEL inherits without this grounding:** The domain modeling phase will inherit "layer-conditional composition" as established product vocabulary and may treat it as an operator-facing concept warranting a ubiquitous-language glossary entry, when the more appropriate treatment is as a framing concept the analyst uses to explain the architecture rather than a term the operator names and acts on. The distinction matters for domain-model concept scoping — the domain model should name what the system does from the operator's perspective, not why the architecture works from the research phase's perspective.

### Finding 2: Value tension #8's binary framing should reflect the tri-position the gate produced

**What is uncertain:** Whether the practitioner's gate-response (rejecting the static-specification binary and offering the runtime-probe as a compatible third position) was intended to expand the tension framing in §Value Tensions, or only to add a new scenario candidate to DECIDE. The assumption inversion and capability floor vocabulary refinement record the third position accurately; the tension framing does not.

**Concrete grounding action:** Update tension #8 in §Value Tensions to reflect three positions before MODEL begins: (1) static default that tolerates misconfiguration and produces unrecoverable first sessions, (2) operator-configuration required — no graceful default, and (3) the practitioner's gate-response candidate — a calibration ensemble that probes available local models at install/startup and produces operator-readable recommendations when any tested profile falls below baseline. The current binary framing positions the design choice as static-default-vs.-operator-configured; the practitioner's gate response established that a third surface exists (runtime-probe at install/startup) that is compatible with both rather than an alternative to either. The update does not require a new gate exchange — the practitioner already approved the calibration-ensemble addition in three locations at the gate; extending that approval to the tension framing itself is consistent with what was already recorded. The gate reflection note should note the extension and why it was inferred from the existing gate record rather than requiring a new exchange.

**What MODEL inherits without this grounding:** The domain modeling phase will inherit a product discovery §Value Tensions entry that positions the default-onboarding problem as a two-option design choice. The domain model's treatment of capability-floor concepts will anchor to that binary framing. DECIDE will inherit both the binary tension framing and the three-position assumption inversion for the same problem, without reconciliation — which creates a minor coherence risk where two artifact sections characterize the same design space differently. The incoherence is manageable but reflects a gap in the discover update's artifact-consistency pass.

### Scope note on lower-severity findings

**Tension #9's research-voice framing** ("local-first as load-bearing value" language) and the **three borderline vocabulary terms** ("externalized structured state," "initializer-then-resume," "tier escalation") are lower-severity instances of the same pattern. They do not individually meet the in-cycle-applicable threshold for a separate Grounding Reframe action — the cost of posing three additional gate questions at the discover→model boundary exceeds the risk of the framing displacement they represent. Feed these forward to the MODEL phase: when the domain modeler reads the product vocabulary table, they should evaluate whether "externalized structured state" reads in operator voice or analyst voice, and relabel accordingly if warranted. This is domain-modeling judgment work, not a discover-phase gap requiring a gate exchange before MODEL can begin.

**Tension #10's literature citations** (Khanal, CAAF, Li et al.) land in a product-discovery tension but are appropriate there because the open question they frame ("how much architectural cost is appropriate to bound a feedback risk that is real but unmeasured") is genuinely in operator-voice — it names a design cost the operator will face. The research-voice framing is the evidence base for why the open question matters, not a characterization of the operator's decision. This is acceptable product-discovery framing.
